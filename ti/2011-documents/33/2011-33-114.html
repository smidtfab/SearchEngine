DARPA's Red Balloon Experiment

This last Saturday, December 5, DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency) held a red balloon contest to celebrate the 40th anniversary of the internet--one of the projects originally funded by them. 10 numbered red balloons were buoyed in random locations around the continental United States. The first individual or team to locate the coordinates of all 10 balloons won $40,000.

Several teams registered for the contest, some private groups and some public. The winner of the contest came from MIT, who located all of the balloons within 9 hours. (The deadline for the contest was December 14th.)

The point? DARPA wanted to better understand the power of the internet and its ability to allow people to work together. Basically, we were the subjects of this technological experiment. Is that exciting or suspicious? Hmmm...
