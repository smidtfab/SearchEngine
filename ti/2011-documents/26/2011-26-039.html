In Search of Quality Control with Crowd-Based Sentiment Judgments

+10-10
+ Bookmark Download Flag Email Add to favorites

Document summary:

Tapping into the mind of collective input on just about any platform has become a very important way forward for a number of people. However, in many instances quality control is not taken into consideration as a way to judge which of participant is making the most valuable contributions and which is not. In this article we learn just how Crowdflower is using its "gold" approach to weight users credibility and usefulness in a particular assignment. This reduces the chances of getting the wrong answer and also getting people involved that may not have the necessarily skills.

Contributor comments:

Some crowdsourcing websites have turned a blind eye to ensuring that participants that are giving their contribution online have the necessary skills and drive to give their best. This often results in information that is useless and that needs to be filtered to be considered useful for particular projects. With the implementation of quality control reigns, it makes the whole processes involved in crowdsourcing a lot easier and also adds credibility to the concept behind it.
