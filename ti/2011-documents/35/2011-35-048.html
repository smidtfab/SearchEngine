Research

  • Crowdsourcing for relevance evaluation Mostly working with Mechanical Turk and trying all sorts of things for relevance (TREC and INEX), document summarization, and related stuff.

  • Semantic annotations in information retrieval
    The special issue from Information Processing and Management has been published: Volume 46, No. 4, July 2010.
    Hugo Zaragoza (Yahoo! Research) and I have organized the workshop "Exploiting Semantic Annotations in Information Retrieval" as part of ECIR 2008. The workshop went very well and we got lots of good feedback. The proceedings are available from the website. We also organized a second ESAIR workshop at WSDM 2009 in Barcelona.

  • Temporal information retrieval. This is the PhD thesis topic that combines exploratory search, TimeML, text mining, and NLP techniques. Advisors: Michael Gertz and Ricardo Baeza-Yates.

    Talk on Time in Web Search at the Yahoo event in Andorra. Last year I was at SIGIR 2007 in Amsterdam, where I did demo the project. I also presented in the Industry day on sensemaking. I did participate on the ESI CHI 2007 workshop, showing my demo on exploratory search using timelines.

  • Mining software repositories (MSR). This is a past project (Minero) about using information retrieval and text mining techniques to software engineering assets for discovery of patterns and other things. Mainly open source projects like HTTPD Apache.
    Publications:
    • Expertise Identification and Visualization from CVS, MSR 2008. Demo here.
    • Database Techniques for the Analysis and Exploration of Software Repositories, First Mining Software Repositories Workshop, 2004. (pdf)
    • Extracting Contributor Information from Software Repositories. Unpublished. (pdf).

    As part of the research on MSR, I did a couple of prototypes (including visualization): expertise identification on open source and contributor analysis on the Apache CVS logfile. A big thanks to the Apache folks for providing me original data.

  • Database-driven information visualization. Visualization metaphors have been around for years but their adoption has been low. The common myth is that you need sophisticated users to take advantage of them. That might be in part true, but the real issue is that without good content organization and structure there is no room for visualization. More info on database and visualization.
