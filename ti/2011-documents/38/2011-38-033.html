NHS patient starts online crowdsourcing service

By Gary Cansell
06 January 10

  • Asus Eee Videophone AiGuru SV1

  • Kazakh PM orders ministers to start blogging
  • London event seeks web talent of the future

Chris Quigley couldn’t stay in bed all day munching bags of Haribo and watching iPlayer. His being hospitalised, however, meant the situation was somewhat inescapable. And so, unable to walk, he began running mental marathons. As his health improved, he wondered, was there any way the hospital could getter better, too – a kind of reciprocal medicine? The answer was an emphatic Why in the World Wide Web 2.0 not?

Two days into his recumbent routine, Chris discovered that King’s College Hospital has WiFi. Keeping this in mind, it took him just a couple hours and a couple of mouse clicks to formulate a plan and bring it into fruition. Delib, the e-democracy company he founded, set up an online patient crowdsourcing service.

Based on a dialogue app developed by his company and used by clients ranging from the Obama administration to Macmillan Cancer Support, the resulting website, helpusimprovekings.org, provides a forum through which proactive patients and employees can share ideas and suggest ways to improve the service provided.

“Laying on my back all day I felt massively disempowered,” says Chris. “The nurses were doing everything for me as I couldn’t move. They were fantastic, but I thought there were some simple ways in which the hospital could be improved. King’s has its own patient feedback mechanics, but they’re all about feedback once you’re out of hospital, not while you’re at the coal face.”

Despite remaining in hospital with a slipped disc for ten days, Chris encouraged those around him to visit the site and get ideas flowing. Artwork to cheer up anodyne interiors, online patient interaction and computer rentals are some of the ideas mooted so far. Once made, others can comment and vote on suggestions, and Delib has discussed with the King’s Patient and Public Involvement team the possibility of implementing them. Five other hospitals have been introduced to the platform and many have given positive feedback.

“I’m a great believer in harnessing the intelligence of the mass,” says Chris, “and I had the ability and capacity to do it; I just clicked a couple of buttons.”
