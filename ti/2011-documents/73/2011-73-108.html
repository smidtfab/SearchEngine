Dapperstraat 315, 1093 BS Amsterdam.

The subject of the symposium is ‘Crowdsourcing in exhibitions’.

Crowdsourcing is the act of outsourcing tasks, traditionally performed by an employee or contractor, to an undefined, large group of people or community (a crowd), through an open call.

Nowadays Crowdsourcing is not only involved in business. Many museums and other heritage institutions also use Crowdsourcing. For example, a museum could use their audience in the process of making an exhibition. This is possible in many ways. Using the internet for Crowdsourcing is not necessary, but it is the most frequently used way.

A very good example of Crowdsourcing is the Dutch project of The Museum of National History  ‘Nieuwe Groeten Uit…’
In this project The Museum of National History, the Foam photography museum Amsterdam, the ANP Historisch Archief and the BankGiro Loterij asked the audience to send in new images of ‘the Netherlands’. Almost 8000 pictures where contributed. There were photographs of beautiful sunsets, windmills and flower fields that could directly be used as postcards for the tourist shops. But also pictures of the new urbanism and the multicultural society. Together they make a unique portrait of the Netherlands now, a source for new postcards but also for an exhibition. From May 21st till July 18th 2010 this exhibition took place in the centre of Arnhem. The exhibition was put together out of all the contributions of the audience. Without those contributions the content of the exhibition would have been missing.
