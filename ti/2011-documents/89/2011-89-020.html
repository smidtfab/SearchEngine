About

Current

I am a research assistant at the Madeira Interactive Technologies Institute and my current work involves research into how user-generated microblogging content (mainly from Twitter) can be leveraged to increase situational awareness in large-scale emergency situations, e.g. earthquakes and hurricanes. To achieve this, an approach is used where information is extracted, aggregated, annotated and displayed by combining techniques from fields such as data mining, natural language processing, crowdsourcing and information visualization.

Background

I have a MSc in Media Technology and Engineering from the University of Linköping, Sweden. In my final thesis project, I developed visualization tools for internal market surveillance and external dissemination of public market data at the Ethiopia Commodity Exchange (ECX) in Ethiopia. Before joining M-ITI I worked in the industry as a software developer designing visualization tools and data mining algorithms for a company in the fuel and energy sector and I was employed as research engineer at Sweden's National Center for Visual Analytics while completing my studies.
