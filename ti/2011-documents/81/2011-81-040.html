This is another thing that bothers me about the concept of mass-rejecting a large amount of HITs...

If a particular requester literally requires you to have an approval rating of 99% or higher to do their HITs, how in the world can they possibly justify mass rejecting the work that gets submitted to them? It's absurd, especially considering the possibility that there may have been an error on their end.

I had to complete every single one of my 60,000 finished HITs individually, as did everyone else who works at the Mechanical Turk site - so why should any requester get to reject thousands of HITs in one shot? To me, that seems completely unfair and I highly doubt I'm the only one who feels this way.

There's no appeal process in place for this type of thing. Some requesters (including a particularly unscrupulous one, I wonder why) won't even respond to messages sent via the site. There's virtually no recourse or protection for workers from requesters who pull stunts like this, and there should be.
