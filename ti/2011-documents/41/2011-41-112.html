Company Spotlight: CrowdFlower

CrowdFlower on GitHub

The company spotlight is a place where we take some time to talk with some of the awesome companies using GitHub Jobs and learn a little more about them.

What does your company do?

Think map reduce, but with people. We also do some traditional map reduce, but who doesn't ... am I right? If you're familiar, with Mechanical Turk you know where the idea came from. We add more volume and quality-control tools to make the kinds of tasks you can do with a service like Mechanical Turk more efficient.

How long has the company been around?

We started the company in December 2007. For the first seven months it was just Lukas Biewald and me, lots of coffee shops, and no paychecks. We persisted through 2008 and kept cranking away on the product. 2009 brought some great angel investors and a spot in TC50. Last January, we raised five million and are currently 30 people strong.

What the what is crowdsourcing?

Crowdsourcing in CrowdFlower's world means tapping into the power of a globally distributed on-demand workforce. CrowdFlower partners with a number of different web-based labor providers, which allows us to access a diverse workforce. In general the best tasks for our platform are ones that have lots of volume but can't be completed by computers. Some examples are content moderation, verifying business information, or ranking search results.

What's your favorite part of working there?

Doing something that no one's done before. It's exciting to work on a product that generates real revenue and has hundreds of thousands of users. Being able to innovate both in the way we deliver high-quality crowdsourced results, and the way we use cutting-edge tools like Redis, Mongodb, and FastCGI.

What kind of projects would a Ruby / Burrito Guru Ninja-Pirate Folkstar work on?

The Folkstar would be working deep in the bowels of the platform ... I'm talking making Ruby scale, improving our Restful API, porting the app to Scala, giving it some Clojure ... stuff like that.

What's the team like?

Hip, young, and sexy. We currently have seven full-time engineers, we are more agile than you can believe. Not so much when it comes to development, more so in our ability to move quickly and lightly. Every Friday, the whole company gathers to demo the new hotness. Our office is on 16th and Mission which is dangerously close Kilowatt, Four Barrel, and great Mexican food.

Best place to grab a burrito around 16th and Valencia?

Believe it or not, I've been burrito'ed out lately. I've made the transition to the awesome Empanadas of Chile Lindo's.
