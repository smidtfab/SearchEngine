This Week In The Profession

  • »Two Firms Fill Key Leadership Roles
    Peal Meyer & Partners’ recently hired Shekhar Purohit to head its Northern California Office. He was previously Hewitt Associates’ Global Compensation Consulting Leader. Meanwhile, CIBER tapped Eric Goldfarb as its new CIO; he previously served as CIO of BearingPoint from 2006 to 2010.
  • »BCG Opens 2 New Offices, Tops $3B in Revenue
    The Boston Consulting Group (BCG) has opened two new offices—in Tel Aviv and Perth—enlarging its global footprint to 71 offices in 41 countries.
  • »Hay Group, Fortune magazine ID Admired Companies
    Hay Group and Fortune magazine today released their fourteenth annual World’s Most Admired Companies list. For the fourth consecutive year, Apple took the top spot.
  • »Huron Launches Mortgage Review Service
    Huron Consulting Group launched a residential mortgage loan review service that supports banks and other lending institutions dealing with large scale, loan-related lawsuits, investigations and regulatory inquiries.
  • »Large IT Consulting Firms Rebounding
    As we wrap up the first quarter of 2011, it certainly appears the largest firms remain well positioned to take advantage of the improving economy.
  • »Coupon Offers Half Off IT Services... Seriously
    In the IT consulting space, pricing pressure is nothing new. But an online coupon for half-off consulting services is certainly a new twist.
  • »Capgemini Teams with MIT on �Digital Transformation�
    Capgemini Consulting inked a three-year joint research collaboration with the MIT Center for Digital Business to conduct joint research into digital transformation.
  • »Feds Accuse Former McKinsey Head of Insider Trading
    Former McKinsey & Company Managing Director and Goldman Sachs board member Rajat Gupta has been accused by federal regulators of revealing inside information about Goldman to a hedge fund manager, according to multiple published reports.
  • »KPMG Acquires Global Outsourcing Firm EquaTerra
    KPMG acquired EquaTerra, a Houston-based sourcing advisory firm with more than 300 employees in 13 offices in the U.S., Europe and Asia.
  • »Capgemini Powers Up Intel Utility Offering
    Capgemini announced a new offering for its utility customers using the Home Energy Dashboard, a device based on Intel architecture that enables utilities to interact directly with consumers in the home.
