The Future of Work

December 9, 2010, San Francisco, CA

Watch Now!

The Business of Collaboration

See our schedule here.

Ten years ago, working from an airport, a coffee shop or from home would have been unimaginable, both technically and culturally. Today, it’s commonplace. Businesses are embracing a 24/7 global workforce powered by technology.

On December 9, GigaOM presents a new conference that highlights the opportunities that broadband and mobility have created for connecting work and workers and the technology that addresses the way corporations operate and conduct business.

Net:Work 2010 will assemble GigaOM editors and research analysts, top business leaders and technology companies to explore the challenges and opportunities presented by a new culture of work. And we’ll look at key tools and technologies that make collaboration a simple business.

What equipment and software does the modern remote worker need to work effectively? How are we combating information overload for workers? Is it possible to make any organization virtual? What will the workforce of the future look like? Participate at Net:Work 2010 and have your questions answered.

See our schedule here.

Next-Generation Infrastructure

To make this new era of work possible, we’ll need new technologies and tools, from broadband networks and wireless technologies to software and devices. Web-based software and free collaboration tools were just the beginning. Is your organization prepared to deliver what your employees need to work?

The Remote Manager

How does the modern executive build an effective organization in a world where technology allows you to employ anyone anywhere? How do you manage staff through the web and deliver work across timezones and cultures? Learn from those on the leading edge.

The Net:Worked Worker

What are the challenges that the modern employee faces? How do you effectively manage staff when they are not in-house? We’ll bring you practical takeaways from some highly successful case studies that illustrate the tools and best practices they’ve adopted.

The Campus of the Future

Is your campus becoming a ghost town? As employees demand and require remote working, different types of architectural spaces and operational approaches are appearing, answering the needs of industry. We’ll survey the various options available and different approaches to make them work.

Resourcing Human Resources

In today’s world, where two years is a long time on the job, we’ll look at the changing role and needs of human resources. How can HR utilize new software tools? Does recruiting through social media have legal implications? Can crowdsourcing play into an HR strategy? How do you maintain the well-being of remote workers?

Questions we will investigate:

  • As a leader, how will you define the culture of your virtual workforce?
  • What equipment and software does the modern remote worker need to work effectively?
  • How are we combating information overload for workers?
  • Is it possible to make any organization virtual?
  • What will the workforce of the future look like?
  • What are the liabilities and responsibilities for the modern employer and its virtual workforce? And how can technology help you mitigate them?

Be prepared for change by being briefed at Net:Work 2010. Get your ticket today!

Who should attend Net:Work 2010:

  • CEO, CIO, COO, CTO, Manager or Executive – Your business is changing before you. Opportunities and ROI are there. Get briefed.
  • IT Manager or resourcing professional – Anticipate the needs of your enterprise as as it evolves.
  • Human Resources Professional – What are the needs of the remote worker and how will HR navigate this new way of working?
  • Product Managers and Product Marketing Professionals – Understand trends and market shifts as you anticipate the service needs of a global remote workforce.
  • Venture Capitalists – The enterprise market is worth $222 billion dollars. Find your next big idea and investment.
  • Entrepreneurs – Build your next venture and structure its organization for future success. Meet customers.
  • Virtual workers – Whether you work for yourself, manage a remote team, or are part of a corps of remote workers, weigh in on the tools that will make you do your job faster, smarter, better.
