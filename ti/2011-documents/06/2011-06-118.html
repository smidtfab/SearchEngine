CrowdFlower Bolsters a New Era of Work












San Francisco (PRWEB) September 15, 2009

Statistical high quality control technologies finally makes cloud labor a viable organization reality with the largest pool of workers ready to deal with your job 24/7

News Facts:

CrowdFlower (http://www.crowdflower.com), a TechCrunch50 company, launched right now at the event.
CrowdFlower is the 1st Labor as a Service (LaaS) offering to hit the marketplace – similar to cloud computing, LaaS is labor in the cloud.
The Business also today announced an angel investment round which closed in March 2009. The round included venture capital firms: Freestyle Capital, Founder’s Fund, K9 Ventures, Quest Venture Partners and Felicis Ventures. And individual angel investors: Alex Edelstein, Auren Hoffman, Gary Kremen, Lorenzo Thione, Steve Bennet, Travis Kalanick and Barney Pell.
CrowdFlower taps into the world’s largest labor pool, over 100,000 workers across 80 countries, and creates a human platform for cloud computing with an on-demand, on the internet workforce.
CrowdControl, CrowdFlowers’s statistical top quality control technology, directly measures worker accuracy and competence for maximum reliability of results and dynamic allocation of resources. It also supplies final confidence estimates in its outcomes – eliminating the require to select between manually verifying all the results or blindly taking the very good with the bad.
CrowdFlower is now open to the public with an easy-to-use do-it-yourself web interface. It automates LaaS to offer these key advantages:
** Speed: Thousands of men and women can work in parallel on tedious tasks that would take any 1 person months.

** Affordability: Hire precisely the number of people you require, and pay-as-you-go.

** Simplicity: Eliminate the want to hire and manage the labor your self.

** Reliability: By tapping into people’s additional time, you can get aid 24/7.

** Top quality: Technologies flags bad answers and assures accuracy.

For web workers, CrowdFlower brings together three things folks naturally want but rarely get to do together – a chance to be productive, zone out and make cash. This is a real way for folks to make dollars on the web.
Try out CrowdFlower right now at http://www.crowdflower.com to get excellent work from real men and women. For a CrowdFlower video demo, go to: http://blip.tv/file/2308228.

CrowdFlower Product Offerings:

1. CrowdFlower WebApp: A self-serve version, instant gratification.

2. CrowdFlower Content Moderation (CrowdSifter): A fully-featured interface for image moderation tasks – social networks, UGC and content portals.

3. CrowdFlower Custom Solutions: An end-to-end answer with a Program Manager advising on all stages: scope creation, trial projects, production and outcomes reporting/analytics.

Background Facts:

What is Crowdsourcing: According to the crowd on Wikipedia, crowdsourcing is “the act of taking tasks traditionally performed by an employee or contractor, and outsourcing it to an undefined, typically significant group of folks or community in the form of an open call.”
What is Cloud Labor: Not unlike cloud computing, cloud labor is a dynamic, scalable way to tap into the global workforce via the Web to work on tasks that are challenging for a computer but relatively effortless for a person. While cloud labor offers inexpensive, fast, effortlessly scalable, pay-per-use work, the vast amount of noise in the results, high variations in accuracy and potential for spammers, present considerable obstacles to adopting it as a viable company remedy.
What is Labor as a Service (LaaS): Labor as a Service (LaaS) takes the speed and affordability of working with on the internet labor pools, and adds a layer of quality, efficiency and accuracy to make cloud labor a sustainable business reality:
** LaaS creates jobs and provides a workplace that is a complete meritocracy: folks are paid strictly based on the quality and speed of their work.

** LaaS provides a price conscious way to eliminate the key lead time and massive overhead associated with hiring or outsourcing by bringing high quality control and efficiency to the cloud.

** To save funds and time, companies’ will look to cloud labor for speed and efficiency and demand LaaS for high quality control and accuracy.

** Some examples of tasks suitable for LaaS consist of medical transcription, analysis, translation, data entry and content moderation.

Cloud Computing Marketplace Size: In October 2008, IDC predicted that IT cloud services will reach $ 42B by 2012 with a lot more and far more overall IT spending going toward cloud computing every single year throughout the period.

Attributable to Tim Ferriss, Author, the Four Hour Work Week: “CrowdFlower has the potential to make everybody more efficient by outsourcing not to 1 individual, but to thousands of people at once.”

About CrowdFlower:

CrowdFlower (http://www.crowdflower.com) gives Labor as a Service (LaaS) by letting clients access an always-on, scalable workforce. Unlike conventional techniques of outsourcing and hiring which require main lead time and lots of wasteful overhead, CrowdFlower’s web offering makes it possible for businesses to immediately access thousands of workers with out even picking up the phone. CrowdFlower’s statistical top quality control technology finally makes cloud labor a sustainable company reality by way of accuracy guarantees and efficient use of workers. Founded in December 2007 by former Powerset (MSFT) employees, Lukas Biewald & Chris Van Pelt, CrowdFlower is headquartered in San Francisco.
