Spring Refresh – new look, new features!

Over here in Seattle spring has started to arrive. Flowers are beginning to bloom, the sun is peaking out and our Requester website has been refreshed!

We wanted to improve the look and feel of the Requester Website so that we could provide more helpful content about Mechanical Turk that showcases how business are using the marketplace to clean their data, categorize items, create and moderate content or get business feedback.



As you’ll see we’ve updated the navigation, created a homepage for Developers and added more information on how to use Mechanical Turk through use cases and case studies. We are also highlighting the robust ecosystem of Applications and Solution Providers who are building useful tools on top of the Mechanical Turk API. Go check it out!

In addition to the Requester website refresh we have also released a few feature improvements – details below.

  • New Worker statistics (Web UI only)
    See 7-day and 30-day performance statistics in addition to lifetime data on Workers so that Requesters can identify which Workers are currently performing well or poorly against past performance.
  • HIT & Qualification Search improvements
    More easily find the HITs you want to work on and the Qualifications you need.
  • Bonus Reasons update
    Workers will now receive a copy of the Bonus Reason when they receive a bonus.
  • API Improvements
    We’ve modified our API to be more standards compliant.

New Worker Management Statistics (Web UI only)
We have made an improvement to the Worker Statistics available in the Web UI. You can now view approval rates for your Workers by 7 days and 30 days in addition to the Lifetime data that was previously available. These statistics are inclusive of the current day. For example if it is Tuesday February 10 at noon, you would see statistics for the current day plus statistics for the previous 6 days (i.e. Tuesday Feb10th through the previous Wednesday, February 4th).

HIT & Qualification Search Improvements
Prefix searching on HITs
You can now search for a partial term such as Cast and find results for Casting Words and Castle.  This change applies to all the words in the title, the keywords and the requesters name.
Search parity on Qualification search
Qualification Search now searches across Title, Keywords and Requester name and supports prefix search.

Bonus Reasons are now shared with Workers
Requesters can optionally include a “Reason” when they grant a bonus via the API, CLT or Web UI. Previously these notes were not distributed to Workers but they will now be included in a notification e-mail that the Worker receives. If the reason is left blank nothing will be shared with the Worker.

API Improvements
We’ve modified our API to be more standards compliant. We’ve changed the way in which we URL encode signatures on outbound REST event notification requests so that it is standards compliant.
We also now support all of the standard W3C xhtml entities within FormattedContent blocks.
Learn more about URL Encoding changes on the forum >>

We hope you enjoy these updates – and are getting some spring sunshine!
The Mechanical Turk Team

How to fund your Mechanical Turk account with a bank account

Many of our Requesters today fund their accounts with a credit card – but we often get asked about funding an account with a bank account. Here’s how:

Part 1: Add your bank account to your Amazon Payments Account



First, you will need to add your bank account details to your Amazon Payments account.  If you’ve ever purchased Mechanical Turk pre-paid HITs you have an Amazon Payments account associated with your Mechanical Turk account. To add the bank account to your Amazon Payments account:

  1. Log in to http://payments.amazon.com
  2. Go to “Edit My Account Setting” in your Payments account. 
  3. Click on “Add bank account”
  4. Provide your account details including your Bank Account Holder Name, Routing Number, Account Number and Account type (personal or corporate checking account).
  5. Click Continue and you will be led through the Bank Account verification process.

Part 2: Pre-pay for HITs with your bank account

Once your bank account has been verified, you can use it to purchase Mechanical Turk pre-paid HITs by following these steps:

  1. Log in to your Mechanical Turk Requester account - http://requester.mturk.com
  2. From your Mechanical Turk account, go to Account Settings -> Prepay for Mechanical Turk HITs.
  3. Enter in the amount you would like to fund. 
  4. Click “Continue to Amazon Payments”
  5. Login to Amazon Payments with your Amazon Payments account email and password. 
  6. Select the bank account you want to fund the Mechanical Turk account.  
  7. Confirm your selection.

Note: It can take up to 5-7 days for bank account transfers to clear and for the funds to be available in your Amazon Mechanical Turk account.

Fixing Dirty Data with Mechanical Turk – Werner Vogel’s Keynote @ Strata

A few weeks ago, I attended the O’Reilly Strata conference where Werner Vogels, CTO of Amazon, gave a keynote.  He spoke about big data – how to collect, store, organize, analyze and share data. In his talk (at minute 13:04) he covered how enterprises are using Mechanical Turk to organize their data. Specifically, how you can leverage the hundreds of thousands Workers on Mechanical Turk to: 

  • Verify your data
  • Correct data
  • Enrich data by adding meta information to your data set
  • Filter data through content moderation

He then shared a common case study of how a company managing millions of business listings incorporated Mechanical Turk into their data management workflow to optimize the accuracy of their data and improve their customer experience. In the scenario he described, the provider of business listings built a data processing engine that automatically sent data exceptions to Mechanical Turk. AWS Office Hours - Recap

A couple of weeks ago we held our first AWS sponsored Mechanical Turk Office Hours. It was awesome to connect with our customers – both Workers and Requesters and to hear your questions. 

In the Office Hours we spent part of the time sharing updates on Mechanical Turk and spent the rest of the time answering your questions.  For those of you who were not able to attend, here is a link to the video (please note that the audio on the video starts at [1:24]), and link to download the transcript (done by Mechanical Turk Workers via CastingWords).  You can also find the slides on Slideshare. 

Highlights: 
We’ve included the time stamp from the video for reference. 

[10:24] Worker Management Overview 
We reviewed our recently released Worker management features and how you can you use these tools to create qualified workgroups. 

[19:52] API Best Practices

  • Take advantage of the notification capability that we've built into our API instead of using the polling interfaces in the Search HITs API or Reviewable HITs API. 
  • Disposing HITs can improve your performance as there is less data to manage for your requests.

[23:30] HIT Best Practices

  1. Clear & concise instructions 
    Provide clear and concise instructions to Workers that make it very easy to understand what the task is and what a good task response would be.
  2. Add keywords to your HIT
    This helps to make it searchable for Workers. Workers search for certain types of tasks, photo moderation, classification categorization, business listing verification, having those types of keywords in the HIT description is very useful. 
  3. User experience or the design of your HIT.
     Make your HIT as efficient as possible, minimizing instructions that push the work down to the bottom of the page. Minimizing scrolling is very important to think about when you design your HIT.
  4. Test & Iterate
    Most Requesters need to modify their HITs a few times before the question(s) and instructions are clear to Workers and the HIT is designed and working properly.  So when you first publish your HIT in the Mechanical Turk marketplace, consider it to be a test. Start by running a low volume of HITs to get feedback from Workers and to validate what the experience was, how long it took, and to make sure that the HIT itself is functioning as expected. Include an additional box that asks Workers for "any feedback on this HIT." The Mechanical Turk Worker community sees lots of HITs and they are able to give very helpful feedback. 

Q & A Highlights: 

[43:26] How should I price my HIT? 
As a marketplace, the going price for a HIT will fluctuate based on basic laws of supply and demand. You determine the offer for your work, and Workers choose what they want to do. When setting the reward for your HIT you should consider: 

  • How long does it take you to do the task? 
  • How quickly do you need the work completed?
  • How valuable is the task to you? 
  • Look at the marketplace and see how similar tasks are being priced. 
  • Test the price in the marketplace with a few HITs – do they get done as you’d expect? 

[46:37] “How do I attract good Workers? How do I find them?"
Take the time to build a good reputation, by being quick and fair in your payment of Workers. Audit work and bonus your best Workers. This is a great way to encourage your best Workers to do more work for you.  We also reviewed our Worker Management features. You can learn more about them in this SlideShare. 

[47:37] A question from TurkerNation: "How should I [as a Worker] handle blocks? 
A block prevents a Worker from working for a Requester. It is individual and specific to that relationship. If a Requester blocks you, we recommend that you work directly with that Requester to understand why. They are the ones who have the ultimate control over that block.

[56:24] A question from Brent Lee. "How many Workers are currently registered to work on HITs?"
We have over a half a million Workers worldwide from over 190 different countries. 

[57:38] “Where should Requesters post their API questions?” 
We have a forum for developers to ask questions about the Requester Web site or API tools. Here’s the link:
https://forums.aws.amazon.com/forum.jspa?forumID=11

Improved Feature: Approve or Reject Assignments while a Batch is in Progress

We recently improved our support for Requesters who want to approve or reject assignments in the Web UI via CSV upload. Now, Requesters can upload their CSV file with approvals and rejections any time after the batch has been loaded, enabling more flexibility and faster feedback cycles. This will allow you to pay Workers so they keep working on your HITs.  You can even republish your rejected work while the batch is still in progress, resulting in faster project completion times. 

We hope you enjoy this improvement!
