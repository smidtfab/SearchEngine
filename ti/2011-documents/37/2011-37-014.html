General Questions
What is Amazon Mechanical Turk?

Amazon Mechanical Turk is a marketplace for work that requires human intelligence. The Mechanical Turk service gives businesses access to a diverse, on-demand, scalable workforce and gives Workers a selection of thousands of tasks to complete whenever it's convenient.

Amazon Mechanical Turk is based on the idea that there are still many things that human beings can do much more effectively than computers, such as identifying objects in a photo or video, performing data de-duplication, transcribing audio recordings, or researching data details. Traditionally, tasks like this have been accomplished by hiring a large temporary workforce (which is time consuming, expensive, and difficult to scale) or have gone undone.
What is a HIT?

A Human Intelligence Task, or HIT, is a question that needs an answer. A HIT represents a single, self-contained task that a Worker can work on, submit an answer, and collect a reward for completing.
How do I get started as a Requester?

Visit the Requester web site at requester.mturk.com and explore the User Guide. Within a few minutes, you will be able to use Mechanical Turk.
How do I get started as a Worker?

Visit the Worker web site at www.mturk.com.

Working HITs
How do I complete a HIT?

From the Mechanical Turk homepage, click on the "View a HIT in this group" link to see a specific HIT. Preview the HIT before you decide to accept it. If you want to complete the HIT, select the gold "Accept HIT" button. Complete the HIT according to the Requester instructions, and select the "Submit HIT" button. After you submit your results for the HIT, another similar HIT will be presented for you to accept.
My work was rejected, what can I do?

A Requester may reject your work if they believe the answer is wrong, the HIT was not completed correctly or that the instructions were not followed. If you believe that your work was rejected in error, you may decide to contact the Requester directly.
How much time do I have to work on a HIT?

Requesters can specify a HIT's "Allotted Time" or how long a Worker can hold on to a HIT. Once the Worker accepts the HIT, a timer begins counting up to the HIT's allotted time. This timer is visible to the Worker on the Worker web site. When the timer reaches the HIT's allotted time, the HIT is made available for other Workers to accept and work on. This ensures that work is completed in a reasonable time period.
When should I report a HIT as violating the Participation Agreement?

If you see a HIT that violates the Amazon Mechanical Turk Participation Agreement including the Amazon Mechanical Turk Policies, you can report the HIT by clicking on "violates the Amazon Mechanical Turk Policies" link. Reported HITs will be investigated by Amazon Mechanical Turk staff.

For example, a HIT that asks Workers for any personally identifiable information including name and email address violates the Participation Agreement.
I get an error message/prompt every time I view a HIT. What should I do?

You may get a browser warning about mixed secure and unsecure content. If you wish, you may disable these warnings by adjusting your browser security levels. Please make sure that you read your browser help pages and understand any implications before changing or disabling any security warnings.

To disable mixed content warnings in Internet Explorer, perform the following steps:
1) Select "Tools" > "Internet Options"
2) On the Security Tab:
a. Select the "Internet" zone
b. Under Security Level, click on "Custom Level"
c. Under Security Settings, scroll down to "Display mixed content" and select "Enable"
3) Click OK to apply the Security Settings and OK to close the Internet Options dialog

To disable mixed content warnings in Firefox, perform the following steps:
1) Select "Tools" > "Options"
2) Select the "Security" tab
3) Under "Warning Messages", click "Settings..."
4) In the Security Warnings dialog, uncheck the checkbox called: "I'm about to view an encrypted page that contains some unencrypted information"
5) Click OK to apply the Security Settings and OK to close the Internet Options dialog
Getting Paid
How do I get paid?

If a Requester approves a HIT that you've submitted, Amazon Mechanical Turk automatically transfers money from the Requester's Prepaid HIT balance to your Amazon Payments account. If you are a US based Worker, you can choose to disburse your earnings to your U.S. bank account or to an Amazon.com gift certificate balance. Workers in India have the option of receiving bank checks denominated in Indian Rupees or disburse their earnings to an Amazon.com gift certificate. All other international Workers can only disburse to an Amazon.com gift certificate.
How do I transfer my earnings to my bank account?

Select the "Account Settings" link from the Mechanical Turk web site. Choose the "Transfer your earnings to your bank account or to your Amazon.com gift certificate balance" option and enter your password when prompted.

If this is the first time you have transferred money to a bank account, you will need to enter your bank account information. Follow the instructions to enter your bank routing and account numbers in the form provided.
What is a bonus payment?

A bonus is an additional grant of money from a Requester. A Requester can choose to pay a bonus in addition to the stated reward for completing a HIT. Bonuses are granted at the Requester's discretion and are usually paid to Workers who do particularly good work.
How do I receive payment in Indian Rupees?

If you are a Worker in India, you can elect to receive a check for your earnings in Indian Rupees. You will need to provide your birth date, passport number, Electoral Photo Identity Card (EPIC) number or Permanent Account Number (PAN). A scanned copy of your passport, EPIC or PAN card is also required. All the information you need to sign up to receive payments denominated in Rupees is provided on the Account Settings page.
It takes 14 days to verify your card information once it is submitted. Once we've verified your information, you'll be able to withdraw your earnings in Indian Rupees. To request a withdrawal, go to the Account Settings page and follow the instructions. Your check is mailed within 6 weeks to the address we have on file.
Why am I asked to fill out my tax information?

Requesters are required to collect tax information from all foreign Workers who work on their HITs. The information we are asking for is the same information you would provide in an IRS W-8BEN form. Please refer to the IRS website for more details: www.irs.gov.
Can I still work if I don't fill out the form?
No, you will not be allowed to work on Mechanical Turk until you have provided your name, address, and certified the required information in this form.
I'm a US citizen living outside the US and I can't complete this form.
Unfortunately, US citizens living outside the United States are not allowed to work on Mechanical Turk.
Do I have to pay taxes on my Mechanical Turk earnings?
Please consult your country's tax authority.
