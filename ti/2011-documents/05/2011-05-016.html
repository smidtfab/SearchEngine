Fulfill Your Dreams Through Crowdfunding

By slv 　http://www.lasvegasownerfinancing.com/fulfill-your-dreams-through-crowdfunding/

Have you heard about crowdfunding yet? No? That’s pretty astonishing, considering that crowdfunding has been mentioned Huffington Post, Mashable, and even the New York Times. It’s not really a new phenomenon, as it started long ago in terms of giving to charity. With every contribution you have ever made to a worthy cause, you’re participating in crowdfunding.

What makes giving to charity different from the way that crowdfunding has been embraced by the cyberspace population? People are instantly seeing a simple way to get the money to do the things they want to do and they don’t have to be a non-profit to benefit.

Crowdfunding, in whatever form it takes, means nothing more than getting a lot of people to donate funds to see something accomplished. Since about 2004, this has been applied to artists, musicians, and everyday people who want to see their projects complete. Crowdfunding can be applied to finish an album, a body of artwork, to complete a film, or in the case of one crowdfunding user, to sell a bit of property for $1 a square inch. Today, there are many reasons that people want to use crowdfunding to get their projects kickstarted.

Some of the different crowdfunding sites are called Kickstarter, Helpalot, Amazee, and Indiegogo, and all are filled with people interested in achieving a goal and creating a successful crowdfunding project in order to do that. Contributions to projects come with various forms of reward for higher and higher levels of funding.

For example, you might list someone’s name on your website or blog for a $1 donation with a link to his or her website. For $5, they see their name in the credits of a film. For higher and higher amounts, more and more happens to make the people donating to the crowdfunding project part of it.

Some crowdfunding sites only take ideas from people in the United States, where some of them accept ideas from the global community. But regardless of the candidates’ origins, what seems to be the most significant aspect of successful projects is some benefit to a community. Projects for building a community playground or giving the aged a place to meet and spend time with friends get almost instant backers and funds, and they often get more than the money they have set for their goal. People just naturally want to help other people, so having this element as part of the project is one way to assure some level of success.

So, if a community enjoys an artist’s music, and they want to see the album produced so that they can buy it, that works better than saying you have no money and need to pay your bills. Crowdfunding is about the crowd, and charitable projects are often over-funded by generous contributors. People love helping other people, so the more people your project helps, the more likely it is that your project will be successful.

To learn more about the different crowdfunding sites, there are many places online that have lists of them. One great source for crowdfunding information is Wikipedia. You’ll get the entire history of crowdfunding there, along with more resources and more information.
