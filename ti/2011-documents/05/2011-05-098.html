Dear all,

I hope I have come to the right place because I need some help with Mars photodata exploration. I am doing a crowdsourcing experiment for my graduation project at the University of Amsterdam in Holland.
Hereby I invite you to be a group of pioneers and to participate in my experimental on-line game Cerberus. Please click [url=http://www.blackshore.eu/Cerberus/play/]http://www.blackshore.eu/Cerberus/play/[/url] and let's explore the surface of Mars together!

By doing this I hope you will learn a lot of new things about our neighboring planet and we help scientists in doing important work. The game starts with a little traing. When you complete it you unlock 9 photographs for you to explore. They are taken with the MRO (Mars Reconnaicance Orbiter) and objects are visible down to 25 centimeters!

The game itself is in continuous development and I am right now working on adding some real- base building. So if anyone of you has suggestions ( I think you probably have) on how the base could look like feel free to share it with me :) )

So, are you ready to launch? I hope to see you on Mars!

regards,

Hans van 't Woud

[img]http://fc05.deviantart.net/fs71/i/2010/197/1/9/Cerberus__an_experimental_game_by_BlackShore_creative.png[/img]

Ps. Feel free to share my project to as much people as you would llike. This game represents my Master thesis project @ the University of Amsterdam for the education Human Centered Multimedia. The game consists out of photographs sent back to earth by Nasa's MRO (Mars Recon-
aicance Orbiter) and made available by the HiRISE programme of the University of Arizona. The game automaticly signs you at random to one of four game- conditions. The more people play the game the more reliable the results will be. The game also tends to raise in fun- factor when more people play.
