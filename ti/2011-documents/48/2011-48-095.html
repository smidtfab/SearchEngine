Law Firm Uses Crowdsourcing for Marketing Help

Posted on 6th August 2010 in Marketing

A divorce law firm in North Carolina turned to a crowd for marketing help and ended up with more effective pay-per-click ads and a new logo that reflects its practice focus. The Rosen Law Firm, based in Raleigh, tapped into “crowdsourcing,” the New York Times reports. Crowdsourcing companies turn their clients’ problems over to a pool of consumers who offer their suggestions or freelancers who generate ideas and compete for the work.
