Find Petrol (Gas) Station Phone Numbers in UK
Post by crabfoot on Sept 13, 2010, 5:13pm

So I tried a HIT from Dolores Labs -
Find Petrol (Gas) Station Phone Numbers in the United Kingdom (Fixed + Better Pay).

It told me I was in training mode, and I had to get four right to get paid. But it brings them up three to a page, so you have to complete six, over two HITs, to get four right ...

The first one was a Tesco station, selling Esso petrol. The program insisted I was wrong to direct it to the Tesco site, it wanted me to link to exxonmobil.

The second was an independent garage near Pitlochry, which was selling Rix petrol until recently, but is now selling Gulf petrol. I gave the garage's website and was told by the program that I should have linked to the Rix site. What good is that? They stopped selling Rix ... I said about the Gulf switch in the additional info.

The third was an ASDA station. The program complained that I should have linked to asda .com - I linked to asda .com/corp/

I gave in and returned the HIT. I had wasted 35 min on a two min task, and the program wanted to train me to give the incorrect answer it already believed.

My connection is slow, and ALL the addresses are incomplete - only just enough to search on.

With a FULL UK postcode and a street number or name, you could do this really quickly, but the postcodes all lack the last two letters, the addresses lack street numbers.

What gets me about these HITs is the bad design.

If they want the names and telephone numbers, why not just ask for those?
But you have to give the website of the operator.

Why does the program make you give the website of the petroleum supplier when it has asked for the website of the operator?

Why leave the supermarket stations in the list? You cannot sell directly into those sites, anyway. Central purchasing, and there is no sense listing the operator as exxon when you want to talk to Tesco in Cheshunt (all Tesco stations sell Esso).

I don't know the trainee who designed these HITs, but I should have their job and they should be Turking.

For that matter, why don't Dolores Labs make their trainees actually work on MTurk for a week, with an earnings target to aim at?
