FAQ

Why categorize products at all?

Discoverability is the name of the game.

Imagine a user on an e-commerce website. She could search for a specific item (say a Brand ABC Chef Knife), or she could browse through a taxonomy to locate all chef knives in which she's potentially interested. What happens if Brand ABC Chef Knife is incorrectly categorized under Tools instead of Kitchen? That's a missed opportunity for the merchant to sell that knife.

What can CrowdFlower categorize?

Anything that needs to go into a taxonomy! E-commerce companies that aggregate and resell products produced by other companies use CrowdFlower categorization extensively. CrowdFlower also works with non-product data, such as user-generated content (e.g., what type of restaurant is this?).

How does CrowdFlower categorization work?

We begin by taking a client's taxonomy and business rules for how to categorize items. We then choose the appropriate subsection of our workforce to target in order to maximize the potential of the crowd.

The workers are asked to categorize each of the items, and CrowdFlower determines the best aggregate answer from those multiple human judgments. CrowdFlower then sends the client back the item and category.

How does CrowdFlower categorization work with your existing process?

Many companies attempt to categorize every item that they resell using internal resources. CrowdFlower's high accuracy enables clients to shift that burden off of their employees, freeing them up for higher-level tasks. CrowdFlower's elastic workforce also ensures that full-time internal resources aren't ever idly waiting for new products to categorize.

Can CrowdFlower build or optimize my taxonomy?

Yes. CrowdFlower works with many clients to discover "pain points" within their taxonomies, which we are alerted to through statistical analysis of worker behavior.

Companies that don't yet have taxonomies can benefit from using CrowdFlower's large, diverse workforce to explore where workers think items go. By looking at the top choices for specific items, our clients can then build taxonomies that have already been vetted by their potential customers.

How is CrowdFlower's service different from that of business process outsourcers?

We apply proprietary technology and deep expertise to the specific process of categorization. And unlike most outsourcers, CrowdFlower does not employ task workers on a full-time basis; we maintain quality scores on hundreds of thousands of workers, which improves our ability to categorize any type of data.

How does CrowdFlower achieve high accuracy and quick turnaround time?

Statistically backed, quality-controlled crowdsourcing technology produces a scalable and reliable workforce that can dispatch a diverse array of tasks and provide near-real time results from workers in the cloud. Multiple crowd workers categorize each item. Known accurate and inaccurate records are seeded into the workflow to assess judgment quality and worker effectiveness. Ineffective workers are filtered out and workflow is optimized for maximum data accuracy.

Does CrowdFlower guarantee accuracy rates?

Due to the wide variety of categorization work, CrowdFlower treats accuracy levels on a case-by-case basis. For existing clients with taxonomies with only one client-determined "right" answer, CrowdFlower consistently demonstrates accuracy levels above 90%. Please contact us about your specific data needs.

How do I get started? What is involved in setup?

Engagements begin with an assessment of a client's sample data and taxonomy and a discussion about project-specific needs and goals. CrowdFlower will require one week or less to evaluate and understand the client data and taxonomy. CrowdFlower will then be able to produce a quote. On a case-by-case basis, CrowdFlower will either recommend beginning with a pilot or moving directly into production.

Results

Challenge

The online retail aggregator has a dynamic product inventory. One of its biggest challenges is to ensure its products are easily discoverable by its millions of monthly shoppers.

Shoppers can discover products on this site using a search bar or by browsing a product taxonomy that consists of nearly 9,000 categories. A miscategorized item is a lost sale opportunity.

An internal team of expert raters cannot scale cost-effectively. BPOs lack the technological capabilities to build custom solutions for such a dynamic task.
