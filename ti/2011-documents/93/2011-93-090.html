Amazon Mechanical Turk Scam

Posted on 02 December 2010 by admin

Share

If you go on Google.com and search for something like “Make Money Online” you’ll find a BUNCH of (mainly crap) articles about easy ways to make money online.

One of the popular ways is to either take surveys online or do “micro-tasks” on Amazon Mechanical Turk for money.

Let’s focus on Amazon Mechanical Turk, and I’ll explain in 45 seconds what it is!

What is Amazon Mechanical Turk?:

This is a website owned by Amazon.com where:

  1. Some people post small tasks….
  2. ….and other people DO the tasks.
  3. For each task you do, you make a little money.

LET’S LOOK AT A REAL EXAMPLE:
Here is one of the tasks taken directly from Mechanical Turk:

As you can see, the task requires us to:

  1. Go on Google.com and type in the search “next day blinds shop at home”
  2. Click on the first result
  3. Copy/Paste the company title from the website into the box.
  4. BAM! We just made a whopping FIVE CENTS! Drinks on me!

Most likely this person is trying to manipulate Google.com to make it seem lots of people are typing in the search “Next Day Blinds Shop at Home” and going to a particular web page. Theoretically this will make Google rank that particular site higher. Whatever….our goal was to make money.

Ok, so we made FIVE FREAKIN’ CENTS. Now we have to find another task.

BUT WAIT…this is going to take FOREVER just to make $1.00.

I could spend a solid hour working and make less than $2.00 at this snails pace.

So why not look for a task with a higher payout? Let’s search by highest payout:

Now we’re talking! Let’s take a look at some of these results:

Look at the three we selected….they almost seem too good to be true:

  • Get paid $19.96 for 35 minutes of work.
  • Get paid $18.30 for 35 minutes of work.
  • Get paid $13.60 for 25 minutes of work.

Seems AWESOME right? I mean, if you work a job that pays $10/hour, wouldn’t making $19.96 for sitting on your butt for 35 minutes sound great?

The problem is THESE ARE SCAMS.

Let’s see how it works….

First we’ll click on the first result and see what this “easy” task is that pays $19.96 for less than 35 minutes of work:

So according to this, all you have to do is:

  1. Click the site
  2. Keep clicking till you can see your credit score by filling in the forms with valid information
  3. Leave your comments below about the site.

THAT’S IT!? They’re really going to pay me $19.96 for less than 2 minutes of work?

Something smells fishy about this.

What you’ll notice when you click through their link is you go to a “Get your credit report” page…and you must fill in all your information including your name, date of birth, email address, area, code, phone number, street number, state, city, social security number and even your credit card number.

Essentially what you’re doing is filling out a credit report request that’s “free” for a 7 day trial period, then they bill you about $30/month after that.

That’s why they can pay you $19.96 to do this, because you’re paying $30 to them EVERY MONTH!!

What a rip off!

This is just someone sending their affiliate link to a credit report offer. What sucks even more is they always “reject” your task saying you didn’t do it right, so you won’t get paid AND you just spent $30/month on a credit report. And what DOUBLE sucks is most of these scams get removed from Mechanical Turk within hours, so you won’t even be able to FIND the same advertiser…you won’t get paid…and you’ll be stuck with a $30/month charge. Welcome to Suckville.

Let’s take a look at another Mechanical Turk scam:

This one promises $13.60 for less than 25 minutes of super-easy work. Not bad eh? Too bad you’re getting scammed here too…

When you follow the link, it sends you through an affiliate link for a quiz about NFL football knowledge, it looks like this…can you spot where you’ll get charged?:

It instructs you to take the quiz, then after 10 simple questions it brings you to this screen that asks for your cell phone number:

Notice in the fine print you will be billed $9.99 every month directly to your cell phone bill for signing up. Once again, they “say” they’ll pay out $13.60 for this, but if they “reject” the work or get banned off Mechanical Turk, YOU DON’T GET PAID and you can’t do a DAMN thing about it.

So make sure you checkout what you’re doing before you complete a task.

With that said, let me give you some really easy tips on….

How to NOT get scammed on Amazon’s Mechanical Turk:

  • Most of the quality tasks (aka HIT’s) that pay over $0.50 will have a “qualification” to take it.
  • You should never have to enter personal information.
  • If something asks for your cell phone number, don’t do it.
  • If it seems too good to be true, it almost always is.
  • If it’s high-paying, it usually requires a skill (such as translating, voice over or computer programming). No one pays you $19.95 for a 2 minute survey.

Most tasks on Mechanical Turk pay very little, so don’t expect to find too many high-payout tasks that aren’t scams. You might want to look elsewhere for REAL ways of making money.

So there you go….watch out my friends!
