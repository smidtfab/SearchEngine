The 2009 DARPA Network Challenge was a prize competition for exploring the roles the Internet and social networking play in the real-time communications wide-area collaborations and practical actions required to solve broad-scope time-critical problems. The competition was sponsored by the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) a research organization of the United States Department of Defense. Congress authorized DARPA to award cash prizes to further DARPAs mission to sponsor revolutionary high-payoff research that bridges the gap between fundamental discoveries and their use for national security. Under the rules of the competition the $40000 challenge award would be granted to the first team to submit the locations of 10 moored 8-foot red weather balloons at 10 previously undisclosed fixed locations in the continental United States. The balloons were to be placed in readily accessible locations visible from nearby roads.
