Website content quality is crucial in many domains, but it is even more critical in the health domain. Users need to retrieve health information that is precise, believable, and relevant to their problem. With the exponential growth of web contents, Recommender System (RS) has become indispensable for discovering new information that might interest to web users. Web site RS are  supposed to help and guide users in retrieving/finding high quality health-related web sites according to the their needs. In order to achieve this, decentralized, intelligent RS automatically give an  evaluation about the quality of the health information sources in the web according to the consumers needs. Collaborative Filtering (CF) are well known as a technique to improve RS with a social feature. In traditional  CF-based RS, entities are recommended to new users based on the stated preferences of other similar users. In our scenarios these preferences are related to the  quality tagging of web contents. We mean by  quality tagging the possibility of tagging web content with quality assessments. Previous research has demonstrated that CF can be improved by using "content" features obtained from the web by programmed spiders. In terms of social web, CF falls in the category of crowd-sourcing techniques, which use the wisdom of crowds theory. Despite their success in the industry, RS, even those CF-based, suffer from several problems. First, the sparseness of the user-item matrix seriously affects the recommendation quality. Second, RS ignore the connections among users, which loses the opportunity to provide more accurate and personalized recommendations.

In this research proposal we aim to extend the previous approach with friendsourcing features. According to Bernstein et al, when information is known only to friends in a social network, traditional crowd-sourcing
mechanisms struggle to motivate a large enough user population and to ensure accuracy of the collected information.  On the other hand,  friendsourcing is a form of crowdsourcing aimed at collecting accurate information available only to a small, socially-connected group of individuals. Our approach to friendsourcing is to design socially enjoyable interactions that produce the desired information as a side effect. to consider recommendation of task based on the social network, instead of considering the crowd.

The main novel contribution of this project is to show that CF plus Content can also be performed using data collected by social networks and guided by the query goal of the search. The application of these features to a health RS can improve the quality of the recommendations according to the nearest context of the user. Specifically, in this research we propose to extend  RS with a friendsourcing CF strategy, this means to calibrate the quality-based CF algorithm with the social network quality tagging.

The participant institutions are: Facultad de Ingenieria de la Universidad de la República, Uruguay (UdelaR); Instituto de Informática de la Universidad Federal do Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGDS), Brasil; Lifia, Facultad de Informática, UNLP Argentina (Lifia); and the Universidad del Cauca, Colombia (UCA). The researchers of these institutions have a rich cooperation experience, being recently involved in Cyted and Prosul projects, both projects related to quality assurance and recommendation systems on the field of healthcare web sites.

As result of previous research work, we have experience in: desig of ontology networks for RS (Lifia, UdelaR); development of metrics of quality for webpage contents (UFRGDS); matching algorithm for relating results of different web reseachers (UFRGDS); domain ontology-based–social network tagging (Lifia, UCA); data mining and RS with user feedback (UdelaR). We will also work in cooperation with EviMed a consulting firm specialized in continuing medical education services and software development for the medical field (http://www.evimed.net/) .to develop a case study in the field of health training.
