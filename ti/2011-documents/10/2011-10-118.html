Is Crowdsourcing marketing’s “Easy” button?

 

I was speaking with Karin Shelley from GlobalLogic last week and she mentioned that GlobalLogic was celebrating their 10th anniversary. As a part of their celebration, they wanted to create a 10th anniversary logo that would represent the occasion and include the seven key geographies in which they operate.

 

Since their internal resource was busy on other projects, they posted the logo design requirements on a crowdsourced logo design site (logotournament.com). Within 24 hours they had several design submissions and within 2 days a final selection was made and hi-res graphic source files provided. All for $250 USD !

 

That immediately started me thinking about crowdsourcing as a great way to get other marketing design work, content and collateral produced with minimum time, effort and cost while taking full advantage of the creative talent and domain expertise that crowdsourcing offers.

 

What do you think? What has your crowdsourced marketing experience been? Are there some projects that are crowdsourced acceptable and some that really don’t lend themselves to croudsourcing?

    Re: Is Crowdsourcing marketing’s “Easy” button?

    Great post on an interesting topic.

     

    What's not to love? The marketer gets what they need quickly and cheaply without having an extensive (read: time consuming and expensive) vendor engagement. And the creative folks use their excess capacity to make a few $ and possibly a new customer.

     

    Our experience with this is limited to a couple of tries, but they've been promising. When you're talking about creative, your exisiting vendors don't have a monopoly on good work. Might as well test the crowdsourcing waters to see what ideas are out there, particularly for projects that aren't mission critical and have relatively wide open specs.

     

    I know this example is in a very different world than most of us work in, but check out the music video for the MGMT song "Kids" and read the story of how it came about in the video description. It illustrates how great band (or company's) fans can be as a creative resource.

      its easy as a concept, but executing it has its own set of challenges.

      1. there is a lot of buy in for the idea, but tha enthusiam peters off withthe campaign.

      2. as it might happen, the quality of inputs may not meet the excellence level

       

      hence, crowdsourcing is a campaign and needs to be managed as such. the marketer needs to be clear in the message, identify the right media and target the right people.

      Once the campaign is over, they must be transparent in the evaluation methodology MUST make the results known to the participants and interested parties.

        Re: Is Crowdsourcing marketing’s “Easy” button?

        Ratnesh,

         

        I think you highlight some areas of caution in using crowdsourcing.

         

        1. Quality - The supplier's true ability to provide a quality service may not be exposed during the supplier selection phase.
        2. Project Complexity - The more complex the project, the more risk that the marketer takes in using crowdsourced suppliers
        3. Project Length - In a lengthy project, the marketer assumes a lot of risk when they rely on a supplier that they have no experience with.

         

        Can crowdsourcing overcome these issues?
