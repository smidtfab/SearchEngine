From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Dolores Labs
Founded December, 2007
Founder(s) Lukas Biewald and Chris Van Pelt
Headquarters San Francisco, California, USA
Industry crowdsourcing
Website http://www.doloreslabs.com/

Dolores Labs was founded in 2007 by Lukas Biewald.[1] to create tools to manage internet crowdsourcing. Crowdsourcing is ideal for large-scale, repetitive yet hard to automate tasks which require an always-on, scalable workforce to rapidly complete.

To demonstrate the power of crowdsourcing, Dolores Labs created facestat[2], to show how a simple task can be quickly outsourced around the world

Products

CrowdSifter

CrowdSifter for content moderation tasks like tagging nude images or obscenities.

CrowdFlower

CrowdFlower, is a general-purpose crowdsourcing app where customers can upload their own tasks to be carried out by users of Amazon Mechanical Turk, Samasource (an outsourcing charity for Afrian refugees) for small payments, typically a few cents per task. CrowdFlower was nominated for the TechCrunch50 in September, 2009.

People

Dolores Labs was founded in 2007 by Lukas Biewald—former member of Yahoo!'s search relevance team and the senior scientist of the ranking and metrics team at Powerset (acquired by Microsoft) — and Chris Van Pelt, a web engineer. Investors include Gary Kremen, the founder of Match.com and sex.com.

Customers

Some of the larger customers include Microsoft, O'Reilly Media and Princeton University
