Crowdsourcing History

This week, I leafed through the book of indentured servants who sailed from Calcutta to Guyana on The Clyde in 1903, the ship my great-grandmother was on. I leafed through it delicately, with gloves on, like I was handling a baby or a bomb. The pages are falling apart and the binding is almost entirely gone. After I was done, there was a layer of fine dust on the table, ash from the quiet cataclysm of turning century-old pages.

And here's the point: What follows is the beginning of a list I've compiled of every woman on that ship, with as much detail as I could jot down in the limited time given to me. If anyone had a female ancestor on The Clyde in 1903, I hope this will be helpful to you in forming a picture of her. You can use the Immigrant Number to get her complete emigration pass from the archives in Georgetown, Guyana. If you have a story to tell, about your ancestor, any lore that might have been passed on about the circumstances of her leaving India, please do let me know. I'll add 15 names every day or so, until I'm done. This should take about two weeks.

1. Azizan, #95589, a Muslim, accompanied by her husband Sardar Khan (#96094), sent to the plantation Nonpareil in Demerara.

2. Anopia, #95590, a Chamar from Sultanpur, no husband listed.

3. Atraji, #95591, a Brahman from Gorakhpur, no husband listed.

4. Aladinia, #95592, a Muslim from Sultanpur, no husband listed.

5. Ananti, #95593, a Muslim from Mirzapur, no husband listed.

6. Bhagirathia, #95648, a Chamar from Azamgarh, accompanied by her husband Barli (#95598), sent to Plantation Nonpareil.

7. Bhagwantia, #95649, a Chhatri from Mirzapur, accompanied by her husband Ramnarayan Singh.

8. Bhagwanee, #95650, a Thakur from Jaunpur, no husband listed.

9. Bhangyn, #95651, a Keol from Raipur, no husband listed, accompanied by her daughter.

10. Basiran, #95652, a Muslim from 24 Pergannahs, accompanied by husband Chand Khan (#95684) and by her sister.

11. Bhagantia, #95653, a Lohar from Gorakhpur, no husband listed.

12. Bhagmania, #95654, an Ahir from Basti, no husband listed. Sent to hospital on arrival, pregnant. Assigned to Plantation Blairmont in Berbice.

13. Bhagwantia, #95655, a Chamar from Jaunpur, no husband listed.

14. Bachi, #95656, a Gareria from Allahabad, no husband listed.

15. Bachia, #95657, an Ahir from Parlalegarh, accompanied by husband Debai (#95712)

COOLIE WOMAN

BY GAIUTRA BAHADUR
I'm an American reporter and critic at work on a book inspired by my great-grandmother. She sailed from Calcutta to the Caribbean in 1903 as an indentured servant. The University of Chicago Press and Hurst & Co. in London are publishing the book, a narrative history of the women who replaced slaves on British plantations in the West Indies. This blog is about my travels in pursuit of their story.
