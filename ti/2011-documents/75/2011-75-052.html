Happy Third Birthday Galaxy Zoo
Clearly this is the year the Zoo went supernova! In other words, it swelled up and exploded, throwing itself all over the place into different areas. Just a few of these new areas are . . .

The Zooniverse
Merger Zoo
Moon Zoo
Solar Stormwatch
Supernovae Zoo

And there is a Papyrus Zoo in the making . . . and who knows what else?

And of course we have switched telescopes, leaving our beloved SDSS for our astonishing Hubble . . .


The SDSS telescope, credit APOD


The Hubble Space Telescope, credit APOD

There is much people news, too - Zookeepers Kevin and Chris have gone to the States; quite a few new zookeepers have come on board; we have an Object of the Week; we've taken part in the "She is an Astronomer" project; we've started a webcomic . . .

And Stellar190 has drawn us an absolutely beautiful card, which will be in the next post

Here's to three lovely years - may there be very many more!

Please add your cards, birthday greetings, comments, any statistics or pictures or projects or anything else I've missed, below!

Thank you so much! This feels like a surprise birthday party...

It's tempting to spend paragraphs looking back at the past, but it honestly feels like the blink of an eye (Our three years is approximately one-onehundredmillionth of a percent of the age of the Universe). It seems only yesterday that I opened my inbox to find tens of thousands of messages about the Zoo.

Instead, perhaps we should look forward - the next year is going to be the most exciting yet for the Zoo, as results from Hubble begin to flow and a new project or three spring up. While we may move on to the <a href="http://www.moonzoo.org">Moon</a>, those of us who were here from the start will always treasure the Galaxy Zoo community. Thank you again.

Chris
