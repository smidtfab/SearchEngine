Historic U.S. site, structure awareness promoted through crowdsourcing, social media

By Charity Riot Project | Published: September 2, 2009

The National Trust for Historic Preservation is using Flickr and Google Maps to enable the public to share personal stories and media about historic sites and structures in the United States. Members of the This Place Matters Web site can add photos to a community slide show — the photos provide a glimpse into the unique and beautiful places Americans call home. The map includes hundreds of historical locations and landmarks.

About National Trust for Historic Preservation

According to the National Trust for Historic Preservation Web site:

The National Trust for Historic Preservation is a private, nonprofit membership organization dedicated to saving historic places and revitalizing America’s communities. Recipient of the National Humanities Medal, the Trust was founded in 1949 and provides leadership, education, advocacy, and resources to protect the irreplaceable places that tell America’s story. Staff at the Washington, DC, headquarters, six regional offices and 29 historic sites work with the Trust’s 270,000 members and thousands of preservation groups in all 50 states.
