What are some of the DARPA approved electronics?

Cell Phones & Plans at 27 April 2009 10:04:40
QI was reading about the Aliph Jawbone 2 headset. I read that it was DARPA approved and I was just wondering just for my own curiosity, what are some of the other electronics that are sold in the consumer market that is approved by DARPA?
AThe latest bit of sorcery brewing in DARPA’s noggin is nothing short of horrifying. They’re calling it the “Self-Regenerating Systems” program, and the name gives you a pretty good idea of what SRS is — the ability of technology systems to repair themselves autonomously. You’ll notice the words “immortal killbot” appear nowhere in the DARPA-approved description:The SRS program is to develop technology for building military computing systems that provide critical functionality at all times, in spite of damage caused by unintentional errors or attacks … The SRS program aims to develop technologies enabling military systems to learn, regenerate themselves, and automatically improve their ability to deliver critical services. If successful, self-regenerative systems will show a positive trend in reliability, actually exceeding initial operating capability and approaching a theoretical optimal performance level over long time intervals.So now that military systems will learn and repair themselves, the robopocalypse is close at hand. The source article seemed to suggest that robots will be physically regenerating, but the DARPA plan is more probably related to computer networks and systems regenerating, not necessarily the bots themselves. This means while you may not see robots reassembling themselves after getting blown to smithereens (yet), you will see them refusing all remote “shutdown” commands and probably killing you and your offspring.Yesterday DARPA released this announcement (pdf), which basically stated that their SRS technology is ready to go, and they want to test it out in some military information systems asap. We’ll try to keep you updated on how SRS progresses, but a more tangible update will probably come in the form of an uncontrollable robot destroying you with fireballs.

Security at 06 December 2006 06:12:27
QI am using the TCPDUMP file of the DARPA dataset. (outside.tcmpdump) for my Intrusion Detection System. But, I am not able to figure how to use the tcpdump files. I am aware that they can be analyzed using network sniffers, and am trying to use Ethereal for the same (I am working on Windows platform), but what exactly is the use of the network sniffer? Will I have to manually enter the data into the database from the network sniffer? I am a little confused with the idea of a network sniffer. I would be glad if someone could help me out with the same. Thanks in advance. :)
AAbout sniffers:http://www.grc.com/oo/packetsniff.htm No matter what sort of communication you talking about, ALL Internet and LAN communication is chopped into chunks of data – otherwise known as packets. “Packet Sniffers” allow intercepting those chunks, viewing and decoding them making possible to monitor every bit of data which leaves and is received by your PC (eventually entire network). Because of this ability Packet Sniffer logs are considered THE most complete form of network communication. It is also possible to open logs made by other application with Sniffer as long as file is made in supported by it format. Go to Ethereal >> “File” >> “Open” and click “File type” with drop down arrow. By doing so you will be able to check what formats are supported by Ethereal. Configure TCPDUMP to save file in any supported format (some will be more complete than other – so do some testing), than load them to Ethereal using mentioned method .PS.Because making sense of data might require some knowledge, page on link given at the top of post contains many excellent references (at the bottom of page).About basics of functioning of Internet and packets:http://www.grc.com/dos/theinternet.htmhttp://www.grc.com/dos/PacketRouting.htmWhole articled quoted in three links is account of successful DoS attack made on GRC by 13-year old would to be "hacker" - very interesting lecture.Front page of article:http://www.grc.com/dos/grcdos.htm
