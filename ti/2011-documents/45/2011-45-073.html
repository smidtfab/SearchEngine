Suitable weight loss programs and products

April 3, 2011 | Author: andrewgregorysmith | Posted in Health

There are numerous alternative weight-loss plans available today, from low-fat and no-fat to low-carb and no-carb, but what are the best ways to achieve sensible, sustainable, long-term progress?

Firstly, it is beneficial to distinguish the differences between weight loss and fat loss. If the weight that is lost is water weight, or active tissue weight, then this is not going to benefit your overall health, or your body composition. It is usally better to strive to lose fat mass, rather than weight.

So, what quantity of fat mass can actually be lost in one week? Are you aware that dropping 1 kg equates to a calorie deficit of 7000 calories? This means that in order to lose 1 kg of FAT MASS in one week, you will need to burn 7000 calories over the number you consume, or 1000 per day. If you are prepared to reduce your calorific consumption by 500 calories every day and complete 45 minutes to 1 hour of physicalcardiovascular activity then you could realistically lose 1 kg per week.

This may appear to be a slow process as some weight-loss plans claim to help you to lose much more WEIGHT than this, but what weight would you actually be losing and can the results that you achieve really be sustained in the future?

On low carbohydrate plans you will be restricting your calorific intake, but some of the weight lost is usually water weight, which can lead to chronic dehydration. The reason for this is that carbohydrates combine with water when stored by the body’s lean tissue, so if you intake and store less calories from carbohydrates, then you might also hold on to less water. This is not as useful as FAT LOSS.

Low fat programmes also enable you to limit calorific intake, but unsaturated fats have many beneficial functions within your body, from micronutrient absorption to hormone synthesis.

By resulting in a calorie deficit of more than 1000 calories each day, 2 changes are going to happen. Firstly, your body may need to use lean tissue to produce carbs for energy and also, your metabolism may become slower. This means that your body’s capability to use calories might be lessened. Both of these changes might impare your ability to remain at an an ideal body composition in the future.

So what is the best method for maintaining a healthy weight and body composition long-term?

Physical activity is a critically important part of fat burning and is just as important once you have achieved your ideal body shape. You goal should primarily be to live a healthy life, rather than jumping from one quick fix diet to another and exercise (even at low intensities) must be a part of that.

With regards to nutrition, your body requires all key nutrients, from carbohydrates and proteins to healthy fat, so cutting out any nutrient completely may not be the best thing for your body. A more suitable strategy may be to identify a programme, or select products, which contains a good blend of carbohydrates, protein and fats and do not encourage that you consume far less than 1500 calories each day. If you would like to create a more significant calorie deficit than this, then commit to more physical activity!
