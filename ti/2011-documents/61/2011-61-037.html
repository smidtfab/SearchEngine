Slogan Your Creative LaunchPad

RocketHub is a grassroots online crowdfunding platform for creative projects. Creatives - including musicians[1], filmmakers[2], photographers[3], theatre producers/directors[4], writers[5], entrepreneurs[6], fashion designers[7], etc. - use RocketHub to leverage the power of their family, friends, fans, strangers, and RocketHub community members in order to raise funds and awareness for particular creative projects and endeavors.

RocketHub enables Direct-to-Fan-based outreach and fundraising. This means that project owners, i.e. Creatives, choose a timeframe deadline and a target funding goal. If the selected funding target is not reached by the deadline, the project owner is still able to keep the collected funds. Broadly, RocketHub shares similarities with such platforms as Kickstarter, Sellaband, and Pledgemusic.[8]

RocketHub was launched in January 2010 and was founded by Brian Meece, Jed Cohen, and Vladimir Vukicevic.[3] The three co-founders are themselves artists - Brian Meece - singer-songwriter; Jed Cohen – actor/producer; and Vladimir Vukicevic – tech-thinker/writer - and thus consider RocketHub a "crowdfunding community for Creatives by Creatives."[9]

Notable Projects

As a sample of the type of projects funded on RocketHub, the following are the top five notable projects:[10]

  • Fry's Gig NYC - Philanthropic Event - Notable Actor/Performer, Stephen Fry's Charitable Organization
  • Bring Here and There to American art-house theaters - Film - Tribeca Film Festival Award Winner in 2009
  • Astoria Music Now! Music and Arts Festival (2010) - Festival - The Biggest Music Festival in Queens
  • when last we flew: MAKE IT FLY! - Theatre Production - Notable Fringe Festival Show in 2010
  • It takes a village to make an album! - Music Album - Led by Grammy-Nominated Musician and Voice of CDBaby Kevin Breuner

Partnerships

RocketHub has partnered with numerous prominent organizations in order to empower and educate artists on the emerging field of crowdfunding:

  • The Recording Academy and The Grammy Awards - November 4, 2010 - Music Box Panel: A Professional Development Event Crowd Funding[11]
  • New York City Department of Consumer Affairs and New York City Department of Cultural Affairs - October 23, 2010 - The Art of the Deal[12]
  • CMJ Music Marathon, Music Dish, and Women in Music - October 20, 2010 - The "Musicians As Entrepreneurs" Official CMJ Music Marathon Networking & Showcase Event[13]
  • New Music Seminar - July 19, 2010 - NMS New York 2010[14]

RocketHub.org

In June 2010, RocketHub launched RocketHub.org with the mission to educate and empower the rising Creative Class.[15] This website contains a database of educational literature - including The Crowdfunding Manifesto. RocketHub.org is regularly updated with blog-based lessons on the evolving topic of crowdfunding.
