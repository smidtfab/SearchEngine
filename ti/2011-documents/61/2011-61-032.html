Why Crowdsource?

Crowdsourcing defined

Crowdsourcing is a way of engaging groups of people to submit, discuss, refine and rank ideas and submissions for a specific question posed by an organization. The crowd may be citizens, consumers, employees, partners or others. The resulting high value information can be a greater understanding of market needs and preferences, a new product idea or enhancement, or a discovery of a solution to a difficult to solve problem. For success, crowdsourcing requires a combination of online technology and human collaboration know-how, a clear purpose and an engaged crowd.

Crowdsourcing – the process, simplified

In simplest terms, crowdsourcing starts with a question broadcast online to a crowd and the host organization comes away with the answer or direction they need plus rich information on crowd interests and preferences. More than just brainstorming or ideation, crowdsourcing uses proven techniques to focus the innovation, creativity and problem solving capacity of a crowd on topics of vital interest to the host organization.

Crowdsourcing models

There are many variations on how to conduct crowdsourcing (sometimes often called "open innovation") with key differences being the role of the crowd and the form of input gathered. In its most open form, crowdsourcing invites a crowd to make submissions, everyone sees what’s submitted and collaboratively refines submissions, and all vote on which are strongest to declare crowd-favoured "winners." What makes a crowd?

Depending on the direction or input sought, the target crowd may be citizens, consumers, employees, partners or others. Most organizations have at least a start of a crowd in their email database, customer or subscriber list, current online community, or their employee list. Crowdsourcing has flourished as many of these crowd members have a strong desire and ability provide valuable input to the organizations that they care about.

Output – What do you get out of crowdsourcing?

Crowdsourcing can yield solutions to problems core to performance and innovations that help an organization to lead. Crowd-generated insight and answers include:

  • Co-created new products and services

    The best ideas for new products, or enhancements to your current products often will come from the people you want to buy them. Crowdsourcing gives them a give you their input, ideas and priorities for what would make them buy more.

  • R&D and technology solutions

    Your crowds can also be a vital source of productive capacity. A crowd of research and technology free agents can help you solve high-value but difficult R&D problems that your internal team may be struggling with, while protecting your IP.

  • Deeper, richer understanding of market or citizen needs detail:

    Traditional market research such as focus groups and surveys limit what the target group can say and often confines who can participate. Crowdsourcing with global pool of employees, consumers, citizens or partners can provide richer input, from a larger number of people, at a lower cost, with the ability to go back to your crowd over and over again.

  • Pre-launch market reaction to new products or ads detail:

    Before the big expenditure of launching a new product or broadcasting a new ad campaign, wouldn’t you like to know exactly how the market will react, or even better, learn how to improve what you are doing so the market reaction will be more positive? Crowdsourcing can make that happen.

  • Brand creative and perception assessments detail:

    From a YouTube commercial, to logos, to green product packaging, crowds have proven great innovators of creative that can bring your brand closer to buyers and fans. Crowdsourcing also gives you insight into what a crowd feels about your brand – including strengths, weaknesses and a pure snapshot of what your brand means to them.

  • Crowd funding recipient or investment assessment

    Crowds can reduce risk in many ways, particularly in funding and investments. Asking a crowd what they think is the best investment, or even inviting them to co-invest at lower amounts is a powerful way to verify you are making a good decision, and help increase the chances of a positive ROI.

In short, crowdsourcing provides a fast and global method of getting the information and market data that organizations need to create and maintain competitive advantage.
