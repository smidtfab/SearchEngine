Crowdsourcing: When You Really WANT to Lose Control

Are you confused? Well, you are not alone. 48% of marketers don’t even know what crowdsourcing is!

Crowdsourcing is simply taking a job that is usually performed by an employee and outsourcing it to an undefined, generally large group of people. In other words, crowdsourcing is asking a group of ordinary people what they think and then using their opinions to help formulate your business strategy, marketing campaigns, and even new products.  Sometimes crowdsourcing can influence every area of your business thinking.

I know that seems unnerving to a lot of people.

The idea is essentially letting your customers, the consumers of your product, tell you what they want.  I mean, what do they know, right?

Well, actually they probably know a lot more about what makes your product successful than you do. After all, they buy your product or service! They know its strengths, its weaknesses, and how to improve it.

Crowdsourcing is easier and more effective now that companies of any size can communicate through social media sites such as Facebook and Twitter. Many of the world’s largest companies are discovering the benefits of engaging with their customers through crowdsourcing.

How about an example?

Let’s apply this to advertising.  Advertising agencies can use crowdsourcing to help them:

  • Express their message clearly
  • Find the product’s unique selling proposition
  • Determine their real target market
  • Strengthen their marketing
  • Create a visual message with a real “WOW” factor

Are you still worried about losing control?

If so, why? What is the real difference between crowdsourcing and a focus group?

With the advent of social media, crowdsourcing becomes easier. Talking directly to your customer allows you, as a businessman, to improve your products, sell more, eliminate uncertainty, and make money. Also, customer loyalty to your brand will improve.

Who would have thought losing control could be so beneficial?  So, relax, let go, and let the crowd guide you.
