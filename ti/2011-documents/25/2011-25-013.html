In this course, open-source acoustic analysis software (�gPraat�h) will be mastered so that students have skills to analyze speech/pronunciation beyond the classroom. Students will be taught about articulation and how it affects properties of the acoustic signal. Students often will be required to submit voice recordings demonstrating their pronunciation. This course provides basic phonetic knowledge that is necessary for work in fields such as speech recognition and speech synthesis.

�ړI

By the end of the course, students will:
(a) have learned the English vocabulary for acoustics and articulation concepts, as well as the basic relationship between acoustics and articulation

(c) know how to use waveforms and spectrograms to analyze properties of speech such as formant patterns, duration, intensity, pitch (including sentence intonation), voice onset time (VOT), etc.
(d) know the acoustic properties of English vowels, including schwa
(e) have had much practice pronouncing English words and sentences

Corpus Linguistics looks at how language is used through collections of machine-readable texts, or corpora ("Corpora" is the plural form of "corpus"), by using computers. Although it is a branch of applied linguistics, the idea of using real examples of texts is now popular among professionals and academics who need to write professionally, especially in a foreign language. This course provides basic knowledge in corpus linguistics with particular emphasis on gaining hands-on experiences in corpus compilation and students' preparation towards graduation theses.

�ړI

By the end of the course, students should show understanding and capability of designing and building a corpus, based on his/her own needs. They should also be able to carry out a simple research project on the topic of their choice. For that purpose, they should also know how to use corpus analysis software such as CasualConc (http://sites.google.com/site/casualconc/).
