Crowdsourcing

09.10.07

Why hire costly professionals when hordes of online amateurs will do the job on the cheap? The peer-production model has proven that far-flung volunteers can create high-quality software and book reviews. Now entrepreneurs are paying the networked masses to do their dirty work. Apparel maker Threadless offers the public $2,000 for witty T-shirt designs. Procter & Gamble gives cash to citizen-scientists who crack tough R&D problems. When the hive mind gets to work, there's honey enough for everyone.
