Fan-funded Music

Monday, March 21, 2011
Listen

Stream m3u

  • Enlarge

    (gregkendallball/flickr)

Musicians are using websites like Kickstarter and RocketHub to raise money for recording sessions, road trips and more. But what happens if an artist pockets their fundraised dough—and then fails to complete their stated project? Crowd-sourcing expert Daren Brabham of UNC Chapel Hill, Chicago Tribune reporter Christopher Borrelli and intellectual property attorney Jonathan Reichman join us to discuss this and other possible legal shortcomings of crowd-funding.

  • Christopher Borrelli's Crowd-Funding Article

Guests:

Christopher Borrelli, Dr. Daren Brabham and Jonathan D. Reichman

Diane Perry (Montalbine) from Union, NJ

I am a jazz violinist and singer, and have a current project on kickstarter for my debut solo CD as I have been a sideman but have not released my own disk.(I also just read an article on kickstarter in the Chicago Trib a friend sent me.)

Although my music is a bit more traditional (jazz standards) and therefore has an audience of generations that even now may not be on the web (my mom, for instance) I still thought it would be a great idea, and it seems so far to be going okay, as I am halfway to my initial goal. Believe it or not, there still are a lot of people who aren't on the net, though, and it is difficult for them to contribute, if not impossible. It is too bad they are left out of this equation, without a way to phone support in, for example, or mail it.

However, there are some projects that seem to be out of order, real estate, mix tapes, and commercial ventures like restaurants that don't seem like they conform, and yet still get on these sites. I am fairly sure it is illegal to sell real estate by the inch, and without a deed.

They also do not adequately explain that pledges are gifts, and limited to a level that does not incur gift taxes, at least, that is my impression. Any more info on tax implications?

By the way, my project is listed under my profile, kickstarter.com/profile/dianeperryjazz or searchable under my name Diane Perry.
