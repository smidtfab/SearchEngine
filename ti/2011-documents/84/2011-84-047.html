Wiki: CrowdFlower

CrowdFlower was founded in 2007 by Lukas Biewald [1] to create tools to manage internet crowdsourcing. Crowdsourcing is ideal for large-scale, repetitive yet hard to automate tasks which require an always-on, scalable workforce to rapidly complete.

To demonstrate the power of crowdsourcing, CrowdFlower created facestat [2] , to show how a simple task can be quickly outsourced around the world

1. 2. CrowdFlower

CrowdFlower, is a general-purpose crowdsourcing app that allows customers to upload their own tasks to be carried out by users of Amazon Mechanical Turk or Samasource (a social entreprise that connects women, youth, and refugees in the poorest parts of the world to microwork). Small payments are paid per completed tasks, typically a few cents per task. CrowdFlower was nominated for the TechCrunch50 in September, 2009.
