Wait! What is Crowdfunding?

In a nutshell
Crowdfunding is an alternative approach to raising finance. It’s been developed over the last decade, principally in the film and music industries. Unlike traditional models which rely on large contributions from one or two institutions [like record labels] crowdfunding is based on raising smaller sums from lots of people, who may be linked by social networks or shared interests.

- http://crowdfunding.trampolinesystems.com/

First off I need to give credit and thanks to Nick Christy of Recycling Shower. Nick and his partner Peter Brewin are working at launching a new company that produces recycling showers. My one goal from all my business ventures is to be able to build my own home some day. If that day arrives I want a shower like this in my house.

I actually discovered them via Twitter (@RecyclingShower) though searching for crowdfunding info. They are attempting to fund their company via the crowdfunding of investments. They had a great straightforward summary page explaining what crowdfunding is so I asked if they’d mind if I adapted it for our use here. Their mention of Trampoline reminded me of Trampoline’s definition above as well that I had bookmarked.

Now where they are seeking actually investments and we are seeking donations, some of the language was edited but for all intents and purposes the credit for the following  belongs to those guys. And thanks to them for allowing us to make use of it.

What is Crowdfunding?
For anyone with internet access, it is now possible to:

  • Find new companies [or musicians, artists, not for profits etc.] with great ideas all over the world,
  • Communicate with those companies [artists, organizations] and buy their products or services, regardless of where they are,
  • Pay for those products or services, in other currencies, immediately and easily
  • Tell your friends about it, immediately and for free, using sites like Twitter and Facebook.

[We deleted a paragraph here directed towards investors and investing via crowdfunding].

Click here to read the Wikipedia article on Crowdfunding. There are a number of companies and websites that have emerged over the last few years to enable Crowdfunding activities, such as GrowVC [investments], Kickstarter [creative projects, and that includes everything from music to design to not for profits] and Spot US [journalism] and these were featured in a recent BBC article which can be read by clicking here.

Examples of Successful Crowdfunding:

  • GrowVC has attracted $12.7m of funds from small investors since February 2010
  • Trampoline Systems, a London based software company has recently raised over $0.5m from many small investors
  • Kickstarter has funded projects with funds received from 70,000 small investors
  • We added some musician specific examples in our post “Reasons for artists and fans to consider crowdfunding“

Also check out the links and info offered up by the @crowdfunding account on Twitter if you want to find more info or current examples of organizations making use of crowdfunding.
