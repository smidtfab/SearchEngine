Suggestions of topics

David Geiger

  • A Study of Human Capabilities involved in Crowdsourcing
    Crowdsourcing is an evolving approach that holds the potential for highly effective and efficient creation of collaborative value between organisations and people in a variety of different fields. It is defined as the act of a company or institution taking a function once performed by employees and outsourcing it to an undefined (and generally large) network of people in the form of an open call. (Howe, 2006) The approach enables organisations, mostly via the use of the Internet, to tap into a bandwidth of heterogeneous human capabilities that are based on individual creativity, knowledge, skills, etc. The goal of this thesis is to study the range of existing crowdsourcing examples in order to develop a comprehensive, well-founded classification of such capabilities and how they relate to different applications of crowdsourcing.

  • Crowdsourcing Innovation and Problem Solving
    Innovation and Problem Solving are complex processes that involve the application of existing knowledge, often in a creative way, to a specific subject. The inherent nature of these processes plus the disposition to benefit from scalability and heterogeneity seem to make this an ideal area for crowdsourcing (see above for a definition). Two examples for successful crowdsourced innovation are InnoCentive and Idea Bounty. The objective of this thesis is to research the applicability and implementation of crowdsourcing approaches for different forms of innovation and problem solving in commercial as well as non-profit fields.

Thimo Schulze

  • Crowdsourcing can be defined as the act of outsourcing a task traditionally performed by an employee or contractor to a large group of people (the crowd) in the form of an open call. Amazon Mechanical Work (MTurk) is a marketplace for work that give companies and developers access such an scalable on-demand workforce.
    Over the last years, research from various backgrounds have written scientific papers about MTurk and contributed interesting insights about research streams like task design, worker motivation and demographics, quality management, economics, etc. The goal of this thesis is to perform a systematic and rigorous literature review about these papers, analyzing and classifying them according to different categories like research method (survey, experiment, case study, etc.), theoretical background (Information System, Psychology, Economics, Computer Science, etc.), or unit of analysis (platform, task, requester, worker, etc.). In close coordination with the advisor, the goal is to provide a thorough and comprehensive overview of the existing literature and to identify further research opportunities.
