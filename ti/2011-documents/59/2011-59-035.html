  1. Hohyon Ryu said:

    1. This paper gives a very practical advice for the researchers of HCI, IR, CS, economics, and other fields to carry out research on crowdsourcing.

    2. This paper covers thoroughly step-by-step procedure of carrying out research on Amazon Mechanical Turk covering HIT designing and worker controls.

    3. This paper does not cover some issues including how much to pay and how to sort out unreliable results.

    4. I learned that there are basic more things I can try as an IR researcher on Amazon Mechanical Turk, especially relevance judgement.

    5. I could not understand how negative rewards work as we cannot change the amount of pay in AMT, and why this paper did not mention rejecting the work.

    6. I want to learn more about whether crowdsourcing is reliable enough to be used for serious research and how it is different from lab user tests.
