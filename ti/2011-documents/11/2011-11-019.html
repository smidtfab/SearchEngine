What does Crowdsourcing Mean to Design?

Sites like CrowdSpring and 99designs have popped up with the concept of offering a client access to "hundreds" of designers all working on a design concept with the hope of being selected and awarded a set sum of "award money".

It's not just mom and pop start ups looking for a cheap logo that have used these sites. Companies like Starbucks and Tivo are using crowd sourcing as a way to refine their logos. Logos that their very brands are built upon.

What does all this mean for the design community? Is this truly the best way to design, the wave of the future so to speak? Does it give starting designers a way to broaden their portfolio? Are clients getting a wider range of ideas than if they had gone with a single designer or design house? These are a lot of questions that I don't have the answer to, but I am hoping this page will attract discussion on the topic.

Good or Bad for the Design Community?

I'll admit, I've participated on crowd-sourcing web sites before, but honestly it left me with a bad taste in my mouth. After a couple of months of doing design work for free with little feedback and a lot of frustration, I decided it wasn't worth my time.

CrowdSpring 

the company I have had experience with

I only participated for a couple months in maybe half a dozen competitions. 4 out of the 6 contests I entered ended up with the buyer backing out and a smaller award given to a designer at the discretion of CrowdSpring. It was extremely deflating as a designer, and I quickly realized my time was too valuable to waste on something like this. Have others had better experiences? Are clients truly satisfied with the results, or in the case of over half my contests, do the clients pull out?
