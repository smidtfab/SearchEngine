Amazon Mechanical Turk: Good Way to Make Money or Not?

Morgan Stockton, Yahoo! Contributor Network
Feb 11, 2011 "Contribute content like this. Start Here."
  • More:
  • Turk
  • Amazon Mechanical Turk
  • Mechanical Turk
  • Mechanical
  • Amazon
Perusing the Yahoo! Contributor Network today, I stumbled across an article on something I had never previously heard about: Amazon Mechanical Turk. So, of course, I read several more articles and went to the site
 itself to see if I could utilize it.

For those of you who don't know what it is, Amazon Mechanical Turk is a website run by Amazon, of course, that provides different tasks (all sorts of tasks that range from translating to filling out a form or testing something) that you as a user can complete for cash payment. The pay range is anything from pennies to $50 (or maybe more. I'm not certain. There does not seem to be a top payment as all of the assignments come from outside sources.)

If you complete the task and submit it, the task creator can accept your work and pay you; however, your payment is made to Amazon's version of PayPal, .

There are always thousands upon thousands of tasks available, which are called HITs. The purpose of the tasks is to test different things with an actual human mind rather than a computer or another machine. When I was on the site, there were 100,000+ HITs available. Of course, all of these tasks won't be available to you. There are certain qualifications that you might not meet or might have to earn first, but the point is there does not seem to be a shortage of earning potential.

There is no maximum amount of HITs you can complete, it seems, but there is a minimum payout of $10.00, which seems fairly easy to reach.

And that's about all anyone seems to know about it. I signed up anyway (it was easy enough; I could use my already existing Amazon account) and have been browsing it a bit, but I have not, as of yet, completed any HITs. It seems as if it will be a good way to add a few extra bucks to the budget, though I don't know if a person could make a living doing these tasks.

I also feel as if it must be a legitimate site. It is run by Amazon after all, and I personally buy things from Amazon and use their site, which leads me to believe that a company with their reputation would not run a scam of some sort.
