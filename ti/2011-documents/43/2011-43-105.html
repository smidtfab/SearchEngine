Introduction to Crowdsourcing

How to Organise 'Crowdsourcing Initiatives' for improved Front End and Creative Performance

Original Air Date: 14 Jan 2010

Were you unable to attend this Webinar?
You can still access a recording of this session - with visuals and sound at a time that is convenient for you. Our archive is offering webinar recordings in MP3 format (audio only) or in Windows Media Player format (audio with slides).

About this Webinar

Companies increasingly use 'Crowdsourcing' to generate ideas from external sources (Lego, Google, IBM, Dell, P&G are well publicised examples of organisations who make the process work for them). However, the effective application of this new type of initiative is still in its infancy. This webinar will provide attendees with a number of tools to effectively organise this new type of initiative.

In it, Jan van den Ende will give several options to improve the effectiveness of crowdsourcing initiatives for inproving the Front End of their innovation process and their Creative potential. However the effectiveness of these activities is not always guaranteed. For instance, the initiatives may generate large numbers of ideas of mediocre quality, which still require high efforts for selection and further processing.

During the session, Van den Ende will discuss actions that firms may take to improve their effectiveness in these initiatives. Amongst others, he will discuss how Companies may increase the communication within the crowdsourcing initiatives to their advantage, and how different types of rewards can be used to improve the targeted performance indicators


Questions:

  • What is crowdsourcing?
  • How is crowdsourcing related to open source and online communities?
  • What are the positive effects of crowdsourcing, and what are the drawbacks?
  • What are strategic purposes of idea crowdsourcing?
  • How can the design of the crowdsourcing initiative be aligned to its strategic purpose?
  • What are motivations of participants to contribute to crowdsourcing initiatives?
  • To what extent can rewards and communication in crowdsourcing initiatives support its purposes?

Webinar Leader

Professor Jan van den Ende
Professor of Technology and Innovation Management
Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University
Holland

Jan van den Ende is Professor of Technology and Innovation Management at Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University. He teaches Innovation Management and Organization of Innovation, and is an expert on new product and service development, idea management, innovation teams and the effects of design. Jan van den Ende published in Organizaton Studies, Journal of Management Studies, Research Policy, Harvard Business Review, IEEE Transactions on Engineering Management, R&D Management, and Group and Organization Management. Van den Ende has extensive teaching experience in executive programmes (both in-company,  e.g. for Schlumberger and in the Executive MBA programme)

Session Outline

  1. What is crowdsourcing? Examples of idea crowdsourcing
  2. Benefits and drawbacks
  3. KPIs for crowdsourcing
  4. Benefits of communication in idea management
  5. Motivation of crowdsourcing contributors
  6. The effects of rewards.

 

Key Insights

In this session Van den Ende will:  

  • Give examples of pros and cons of crowdsourcing initiatives
  • List potential KPIs for crowdsourcing initiatives
  • Give design tools for crowdsourcing initiatives
  • Provide insight on how these tools can contribute to the KPIs.

By attending you will learn how to:

  • Develop potential targets of crowdsourcing initiatives for your firm
  • Develop appropriate rewards and communication schemes for your initiative

Who should attend?

Innovation managers, directors and managers of product or service development, product managers, VP strategy, VP technology, Marketing managers, R&D managers, new business development managers.
