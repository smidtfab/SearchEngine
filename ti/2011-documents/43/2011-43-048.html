  1. Michael Anderson said:

    Michael Anderson

    1. The authors describe the development of a system for accurate image search combining automated search with human validation using algorithms they developed to optimize accuracy, latency, and cost.

    2. The paper excels in the detailed review of the algorithm construction and the experimental validation of the models.

    3. The only weakness is the limitation of four image categories and the absence of a formal hypothesis and variable control methodology to avoid the HARK potential (hypothesizing after results are known).

    4. An exciting concept for me was the previously unrealized potential to combine automated search with human search on the basis of automated results and the implication that crowdsourcing offers the fast response of parallel processing with the low cost of serial processing.

    5. I had some difficulty understanding the probability density functions used to build the delay models.

    6. The authors argue that the predictive and decision-making (automate versus human) algorithms offer potential beyond image search, and while I agree that the concepts can be extended to audio, I would like to explore the application of this approach to non-multimedia non-search sequential computations, particularly one which could dynamically alter human task assignment based on first responses.

  2. Jörn Klinger said:

    1. Summary

    This paper presents a way of using crowdsourcing for searching the web for images matching the one's one has just taken on a mobile phone.

    2. What you thought its greatest strength was

    The paper went into great detail when comparing different parameters and their effect on the quality, duration and cost of the search. The search function that is used was also explained in sufficient technical detail, while still being easily understandable.

    3. What you thought its greatest weakness was

    The introduction failed to make clear why such an application would be useful, hence the authors lost me in the later parts of the paper.

    4. Something you learned from it

    The paper gives a good template for, low-duration, crowd-aided search.

    5. Something you did not understand

    Why would is it desirable to take a photo of something and then find an image of the same object on the web?

    6. Something you would like to know more about

    It would be interesting to see the presented system at work in other areas, e.g. face recognition.

  11. Adriana K said:

    Adriana K.

    1. This paper presents a method for improving image search results (when the query is also an image) by obtaining validation from humans of the images that are automatically returned from a search, where validation tasks are resubmitted based on the conclusions that can be drawn from the validation results already obtained and a prediction of the delay and content of future responses.

    2. The greatest strength of this paper is that it offers a technique for predicting the delay of the humans’ responses based on training data and currently received responses, as well as predicting the actual result (yes/no) of these responses, and thus enables an active approach to crowdsourcing (as opposed to simply submitting requests and waiting for responses).

    3. The greatest weakness of this paper, in my humble opinion, is that despite the good results, it is not perfectly clear how good the algorithm for predicting delay is—it seems to me that one cannot very reliably predict future responses based on just the first returned response, and I am not sure what sort of training data one has to obtain since every image is different, and the only images that can help in training are images of similar difficulty, which is not really measured.

    4. I learned that it is better to separate validation tasks into single pairs of query and candidate image, rather than presenting the humans with multiple such pairs, since smaller tasks get completed faster and more accurately.

    5. I did not understand what sort of training data is used for predicting the results of validation and whether this data is specific to the query image or its class.

    6. I would like to learn more about whether it is really true that the probability of some given delay and of some validation content are independent, and if not, in what sort of cases where they might be dependent.
