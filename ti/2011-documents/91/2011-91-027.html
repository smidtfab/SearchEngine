Mechanical Turk
make your paper surveys run more efficiently!
As technology doesn’t stand still, the CheckMarket team is continuously looking for ways to ease your life. That’s why we have recently launched our Mechanical Turk Tool. This application is specifically developed to improve the processing of open answers from paper surveys.

If you have ever had to do it yourself, you will know that processing open answers can be a hell of a job, especially when you are performing a multilingual international survey. This often can be very time consuming, and will slow down the analysis of your results considerably. CheckMarket’s Mechanical Turk tool offers the perfect solution.

The application is totally free to use and you will not incur any extra costs. On the contrary, it will save you a lot of time and hence money.

 What does the CheckMarket Mechanical Turk tool do?

CheckMarket Mechanical Turk is a very user friendly tool for the input of open answers coming from paper survey forms. Your respondents complete a paper questionnaire. This is then sent to CheckMarket for processing. We make a scan of the forms. Answers to closed questions are recognized using OMR (Optical Mark Recognition) and automatically added to the online results. The handwritten responses to open questions are stored as images and added to a CheckMarket Mechanical Turk project.

Different users are then assigned to the project and can simultaneously work on the transcript. On their screen they get to see the question, an image of the handwritten response and an open text field. In this field the user then simply types the text the respondent wrote. The answers will be available in real time in CheckMarket’s analysis tools.

 Advantages

Fast processing: Because multiple users are simultaneously engaged in a project, processing of your survey results will obviously run much quicker.

No double work: The probability of double work is excluded. Suppose a user has just entered question 1, he or she will automatically jump to question 3 if another user is currently completing question 2. Each user will be assigned the next available field.

Fewer errors: Since the answer fields are automatically linked to the right question, the risk of incorrect data input reduces considerably.

Cost saving: Mechanical Turk can be perfectly used by your own employees for the processing of paper surveys, or you can outsource this at low cost to an external party. For multilingual surveys you may seek the assistance of local employees of your company that master the language.

 Practical information

If you want to use the CheckMarket Mechanical Turk tool for your paper survey, just let us know when you send your project request. You can mail a list of users (names and email addresses), and based on that we can send them their personal login details.

Because the application is very basis and user friendly, the users can get to the job immediately, without any training. Furthermore, CheckMarket offers the use of this application completely for free!

 Did you know?

The name Mechanical Turk comes from the Mechanical Turk chess-playing machine. Invented in 1770 by the Hungarian nobleman Wolfgang von Kempelen it was presented as a mechanical chess machine. In fact this was a hoax, since a real small chess player was hidden inside the machine.

The term Mechanical Turk is used nowadays in general for web services where people are enabled to carry out tasks not suitable for a computer (Human Intelligence Tasks).
