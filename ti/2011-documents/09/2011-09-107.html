LinkedIn Buys Business Card Digitizer CardMunch



We don't get too excited about business cards. But a lot of people seem to organize their lives around them.

Today LinkedIn announced that it had bought business card transcription service CardMunch for an undisclosed amount. The deal comes on the heals of news that LinkedIn is preparing its IPO.

CardMunch transcribes business cards using your mobile device. Users just point and capture a card, and the contact is returned to their phone right away with 100% accuracy guaranteed using a crowd-sourced human workforce by Mechanical Turk.

CardMunch business card scanning services cost $2.99 for the app and $0.25 cents per card after the first 10 cards. But now it will be offered for free with a refund given to people with pre-existing credits. Apparently, LinkedIn is going to write down the cost as getting new contacts into the system is cost effective.

CardMunch was founded in 2010 and is backed by K9 Ventures, which has invested in another Mechanical Turk startup Crowdflower. Cardmunch was co-founded by CTO Sid Viswanathan, who previously founded Rolotrak, a contact management service that launched at the TechCrunch 50. CEO Bowei Gai was CEO of mobile photo startup Snapture Labs.
