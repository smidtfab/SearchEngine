Emerging Technologies Update - 11th December 2009

The ET update is back for this and one more before Christmas then back again in the new year.

It looks like 2010 could see an easing of online gambling restrictions in the US. This report suggests there’s some heavy lobbying going on to relax laws next year. As always, there are opponents, but the potential tax revenues could be the sweetener to help push things through Congress http://bit.ly/8IyTGI   

Just in time for Christmas, a new Internet-enabled TV is about to go on the market. The iViewer will enable online programmes to be viewed directly via a version of the iPlayer modified for TV sets rather than via a set-top-box. It will retail exclusively at Marks & Spencer till March next year, retailing at £499. http://bit.ly/4ta9CU

On a similar note, Cisco has just launched FlipShare TV, a small set-top-box which connects via Wi-Fi to a PC or Mac to stream programs via the TV set which are stored on the computer. This development is part of their strategy to enter the consumer market, having bought FlipCam (the mini IP-enabled camcorder) earlier in the year. http://bit.ly/5a4N8R   See also http://bit.ly/2kY0ga

PC maker Dell has twittered its way to US$6.5M in sales through marketing on the social networking site, though quite how it tracks back sales to Twitter isn’t clear. It says it’s been a very lucrative ROI for the outlay involved in a small number of staff spending part of their time tweeting.  Proof that social network marketing really works? http://bit.ly/8Sxoda

Another story to highlight the power of social networks – this time an experiment using social networks to solve very large problems quickly. The US national defense agency DARPA offered a US$40,000 prize to whoever could find 10 large red balloons randomly placed all over the United States in the fastest time, staged over a weekend. The idea was to use social networks as a communications tool to locate the balloons. Out of 4,300 teams, MIT won the prize, finding all balloons in under 9 hours.  http://bit.ly/874F5G

The latest Android Challenge winners have been announced, and the winning app isn’t that inspiring from what I can tell; an app enabling you to send late-night voice-mails. Huh? Android developers are increasingly frustrated at how difficult it is to make good money from Android app development, and the increasing range of devices supported is also making app dev more difficult. http://bit.ly/7D86Zz

Picture search has arrived. Google has just released a new downloadable Android app – Google Goggles. Snap a photo with your smartphone and use it as the input to a google search. Google admits “it’s not quite perfect yet” and the app still needs refining, but it will only get better. http://bit.ly/52wXfp

Vague rumours that Amazon might be planning a move onto the high street http://bit.ly/8eVAS6

Apple has just compiled its favourite iPhone apps of 2009, and its top sellers. For the latter, an air-traffic control simulator came out on top http://bit.ly/5DNHIE

And finally - a list of the top 10 viral (twitter) sensations of 2009...  X-factor’s Susan Boyle is currently in 2nd place! http://bit.ly/8P7QFD

Happy reading!
