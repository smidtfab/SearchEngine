Video: Anna Wintour In Bloomberg Game Changers

April 2, 2011

{ 0 comments }

It is common knowledge that if you want a career in the fashion industry, you will have to work for it. Also common knowledge: it is a long way to the top. Still, if ever there was someone to look to for inspiration, it may be Vogue editor in chief Anna Wintour, which is why you should immediately cancel your plans and watch this 25-minute video by Bloomberg. It chronicles Wintour’s career and rise to the top of the fashion industry—from dropping out of high school to taking the helm at Vogue to becoming the power player that she is today. Not only is it inspiring, but there are plenty of interesting lessons to learn along the way. Plus, you will hear from RJ Cutler, the director of The September Issue, as well as Tom Florio, the former publishing director of Vogue, and Chuck Townsend, the chief executive officer of Conde Nast Publications Inc. Ready? You can watch the full video inside!

Rescue Beauty Relaunches Four Popular Nail Polish Shades

April 1, 2011

{ 1 comments }

Crowdsourcing is becoming increasingly popular among fashion and beauty brands—after all, what better way to find out what your customers want than by asking them? In November, nail polish brand Rescue Beauty Lounge jumped on the crowdsourcing bandwagon and asked its customers to vote on the four nail polishes hues they would like to see relaunched. Well, dear readers, the results are in, and the outcome is a lineup of pretty hues that will fit right in to your spring beauty regime. The “Bring It Back” collection includes (from left to right): Bikini Bottom, a pale aqua blue hue; Purple Haze, a vivid violet hue; Bruised, a plum-brown hue; and, Black Russian, a black hue with red shimmer. The catch? Clearly these nail polishes are popular, which means they will sell out quickly. Happily, you can pre-order your bottle from RescueBeauty.com now through Sunday, April 3, at 1pm. Each bottle retails for $18.

Miu Miu Launches Minibag Charms

April 1, 2011

{ 0 comments }

Brightly-colored miniature bags are a key piece this spring—as we mentioned in this morning’s feature, Top Buys: Spring 2011 Essentials—but Miu Miu has taken the idea one step further this season. The Italian fashion label has just launched a collection of miniature handbag charms that are petite replicas of Miu Miu’s most iconic handbags. H&M Unveils Fashion Against Aids 2011 Collection

March 31, 2011

{ 0 comments }

A slew of celebrities—including Selma Blair, Keri Hilson, Sky Ferreira, Nikki Reed, and Ginnifer Goodwin—have teamed up with H&M on the retailer’s annual Fashion Against Aids campaign, an initiative that helps spread the message of safe sex and HIV/AIDS prevention to young people around the world. For 2011, H&M decided to create a unisex collection; you’ll find utilitarian anoraks, jumpsuits, slouchy tees, jersey sweaters, and button-down shirts, among other things. “I think it’s really fitting that this collection is androgynous in style since the disease affects both men and women,” says Selma Blair. To support the Fashion Against Aids campaign, head to your nearest H&M store on April 26. Don’t forget—25% of sales will be donated to the charity Designers Against AIDS (DAA) and various other international HIV/AIDS prevention projects.
