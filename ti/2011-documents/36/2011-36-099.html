Translation Made Easy

Posted on October 13, 2010 by admin

Professional translations can be a tricky thing. Certainly a business can’t simply go to a tool that uses only machine translation and hope to get a high quality, accurate, and professional product. Instead, they reach out to services that offer professional translations. However, it may be difficult to find one that can perform fast and be affordable all the while giving you a high quality product. Luckily, advancements in the field have made translation easier.

Now it easier, faster, and more affordable than ever to get the professional translation you need. And I’m not talking about from a website that uses only machine translations and no human input.

Here are three helpful translation tools that use both human services and technology to produce the best translations you can get. Anyone with professional translation needs can take advantage of these services. It’s translation made easy!

Imagine that you need to e-mail an important document to an overseas client, let’s say into Germany. However, the document needs to be translated in German. Well now that translation has been made easy you can do that without wasting time. There are services out there that will translate e-mails for you and send them to your client once done, and it’s fast!

Today’s world is a busy one. You can’t always be in the comfort of your office, sometimes you need to be on the move. What if this happens and you need a translation? No problem, all you need is a smart phone. Getting professional translations no longer requires you to be sitting in front of a computer, now you can get translations on the go!

Social media can play a big part in any businesses attempt to land customers. It’s essentially free advertising. But why only advertise to people in one language? Now it can be done easily over Twitter in multiple languages. Twitter has become a powerful tool for reaching out to customers and with the option to tweet in multiple languages the potential of the social media tool grows exponentially.

So if you are a business that needs help when it comes to getting fast, affordable, and high quality translations then these tools are what you are looking for.

The Truth About Crowdsourcing

Posted on September 27, 2010 by admin

If you are looking for information on what exactly crowdsourcing is then you’ve come to the right place. In this post I am going to look at what crowdsourcing is. Next, I will talk about why it is an important tool. Finally, I’m going to show what companies use it for.

First, let’s talk about what crowdsourcing is.

Crowdsourcing is basically using the public to help you with business problems and input. Instead of outsourcing and spending tons of time on a problem you simply look to the public for their advice and thoughts on how to fix a problem. For instance a website that reviews movies might let the users of the site be the reviewers instead of hiring people to write the reviews. This helps portray what the general public actually thinks of the movie and saves the site money!

Next, let’s talk about why crowdsourcing is an important tool.

Suppose that you ran the review website I mentioned above. Sure you could hire professional writers to review the movies, but that would cost you money. Now imagine you just let users of the site write their own reviews on the movies. This wouldn’t cost you anything and at the same time make the users feel a more personal connection to the site. It is a classic example of getting customer input and at the same time building your site!

With the Internet crowdsourcing is easier than ever because you can connect thousands of people to complete basic tasks that are needed for your business  for little to no cost. It also saves time so that you and your staff can focus on more important things like bettering the SEO of the website. Also it lets you get customer feedback quickly and cheaply. At the same time it gives the customer the feeling that he has helped you in a personal way, and many times the feedback does.

Finally, let’s look at how some companies use crowdsourcing to their advantage.

Many big businesses and small businesses alike use crowdsourcing. They use it because it is cheap and effective. For instance, Threadless.com lets users submit designs for t-shirts, let’s users vote on the designs, and based on those votes they choose which shirts to make. By crowdsourcing they eliminated needing a team of designers, choosing instead to let the people choose what they want to be made. Speaklike.com also uses crowdsourcing to help its customers get translations in a quick and efficient matter. By integrating crowdsourcing and translation software it helps businesses save time and money on their translation needs.

Crowdsourcing is a helpful tool whether you are a small or big business.

So there you go, those are the essentials of crowdsourcing and why it is important. First, what exactly is crowdsourcing. Next, why it is important. Finally, how some examples of how companies use it to help themselves and their customers. Now you are ready to use crowdsourcing to help yourself!
