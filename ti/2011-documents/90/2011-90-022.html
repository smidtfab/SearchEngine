Changing Logo Design Industry – Increase demand for Quick Logos!
Sep 15, 2010 | Categories: Crowdsourcing, Logo Design, Logo Design Contest | Written By: Sam Anderson
 

Every industry changes with the passage of time and logo design is no exception to this phenomenon. After all…change is the only constant in this world. Each industry has to cope with the challenges of changing environment and dynamic consumer preferences. Those who do not accept or implement the change in their business, lag behind. That is the rule of thumb, follow it and you will progress.

The logo design field has witnessed several transformations over the past decade or so. The most visible of the changes are the many logo design trends evolving out of the industry. Apart from that, the logo designers and clients alike have accommodated for the mounting demand for quick and high-quality logo designs.
 

How has the Logo Design Industry Evolved?
 

1) Demand for quick logo design solutions:

Initially when the logo design industry was in its adolescent stage, it used to take weeks, sometimes even months to get a logo design made. But with the increasing spawn of businesses and entities, the need for quick logo design solutions has become a prime requirement. Nowadays, logo designers and clients alike prefer to keep the logo design process as short as possible so that quick and quality logos are produced.

 
 

2) Shift from niche pricing to flexible pricing:

The logo design field used to be a highly niche field free of cut-throat competition and struggles. The prices were dictated by only a few who had the first-mover advantage. Logo design prices for both large and small businesses used to be homogenous, without the need for catering segments separately. Now with the rapid growth in the small and medium size businesses, the demand is shifted from niche pricing to flexible pricing.
 

3) Inclination for Free Logo Making:

Previously there was no such concept on online logo design. But as the industry progressed, new and unique services have started to surface. One of the prime concepts that are increasingly gaining popularity is the free logo making tools available online. Free logo maker aids small businesses in creating customized identities for them in the quickest possible time. The popularity of Do-It-Yourself (DIY) is not something originating in the logo design field. Various trades now cater DIY tools to facilitate clients with a better experience.
 
 

4) Rapid move from custom-made logos to crowdsourcing:

One of the most rapid shifts that we see in the logo design industry is the move from custom-made logos to crowdsourcing and logo design contests. This is not a new or unraveling concept. It has been there in the general trade for quite some time under the name of “outsourcing”. Crowdsoucing is just a modified version of outsourcing. The advantages of crowdsourcing as an alternative of custom-made logos are reduced costs, increased efficiency and wide variety of designs to choose from. It even supports aspiring graphic designers who need a platform to boost their careers.
 
What do you think about the changing trends in logo design industry, specifically the increasing number of crowd sourcing sites? How do you think it will evolve further?
