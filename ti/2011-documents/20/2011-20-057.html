How to Ask Amazon Mechanical Turk HIT Questions

As an Amazon Mechanical Turk HIT requester, I receive e-mails from fellow Amazon Mechanical Turk HIT workers. Because all of the communication between workers and requesters are brokered through Amazon Mechanical Turk, it's not transparent what each party sees. Today, I like to show what a requester see from an e-mail communication and provide tips on how to communicate efficiently. The following is a real example of an e-mail from a fellow worker (sensitive information removed or substituted):

Subject: Regarding Amazon Mechanical Turk HIT Type [HIT Type ID]
Date: Friday, May 14, 2010 7:13 AM
From: [worker name and e-mail address]
To: [requester name and e-mail address]
Greetings from Amazon Mechanical Turk,
An Amazon Mechanical Turk user is contacting you from the Amazon
Mechanical Turk website.  Please review the message below and respond to
the message as you see fit.
Sincerely,
Amazon Mechanical Turk
http://requester.mturk.com
1200 12TH AVE South, SUITE 1200
SEATTLE, WA 98144-2734 USA
Message from [work name and e-mail address]
---------------------------------
Customer ID: A2HPJL6DN3QNJY
I am interested in it. Could you brief me on that.
---------------------------------

From this message it is obvious that this worker is interested in working on a HIT, but needs some additional information to get going. And as a requester, it's my best interest to provide the necessary information to the worker to generate the best result. Unfortunately, there a number of missing information from this worker's e-mail.

1. What hit is this worker talking about? Although the subject of the e-mail has the HIT type listed, it is just a random sequence of numbers and letters. Most requesters, including myself, have no clue on how to associate this type to a HIT description. It's likely we sent out hundreds of HIT's at once. And we are likely too busy to determine the actual HIT in question.

2. What is the worker's specific question? If the worker has been more specific in this area, the requester may be able to guess the HIT or provide instructions to overcome a road-block. Maybe the HIT description is worded incorrectly or lacked information. But without providing specifics to the problem at hand, nothing could be done.

In the future, when you communicate with a requester as a worker, please consider the information that is pass through the Amazon Mechanical Turk infrastructure. And you can really make the process more efficient by providing specific details up front.
