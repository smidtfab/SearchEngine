Fortune 200 Execs: Crowdsourcing Helps Corporate Social Responsibility

Posted by: Neil James // February 16th, 2011

Despite a common perception that large organizations are soulless whales that mindlessly search for the plankton of profit, big brands are making more of a commitment than ever before to philanthropy and social responsibility. Beyond simply cutting a check to a non-profit in return for naming rights, brands are employing strategic partnerships and sophisticated marketing tactics to ensure their charitable activities have maximum impact.

A new survey by Weber Shandwick and KRC Research, as chronicled by Zachary Sniderman in an article for Mashable, What Value is Crowdsourcing to Corporate Social Responsibility, explored the impact of digital marketing tactics on CSR efforts. Interestingly, executives found crowdsourcing more useful than social media! Among 216 executives at Fortune 200 companies, 95 percent found that crowdsourcing helped corporate social responsibility programming while only 59 percent felt social media had a positive impact on communications with customers. Despite this difference, however, only 44 percent of executives reported using crowdsourcing to generate ideas and spur decision making whereas 72 percent reported their brands actively used social media in CSR efforts.
