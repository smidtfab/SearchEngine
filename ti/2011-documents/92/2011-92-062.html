Crowdsourcing Portals – Make Some Cash While Your Other Freelance Tasks Are Scarce

Written on March 4, 2011 by Gman in Crowdsourcing Portals, Make Money Online

Image Source

The job of a freelancer is not at all easy. While it is true that you do not have a boss nagging you constantly and calling at the most inopportune moments asking for that report to be finished, this freedom requires a price to pay: inconsistent work. From February to June you may be swamped with workloads, but the rest of the year you have to fight hard just to make ends meet. Sometimes, you will accept working 50% off just to make sure you can pay your bills. So what is there to do? How could a crowdsourcing online environment possibly help?

Choose according to your skills

If you are a freelance translator, go to oDesk for work. If you are a freelance programmer oDesk or Freelancer.com might be the answer to your prayers. For freelance photographers, customAdArt is the place to be. Web designer or animator? Poptent is the solution.

These are only a few suggestions, but the list could definitely continue. You just need to make sure that you are searching in the right place for a job. It is not easy to keep abreast with all these huge crowds present on these crowdsourcing portals, but if you are a true professional, you are going to make yourself some steady business partners in here.

Build yourself a nice presence

Image Source

On most of the crowdsourcing websites, you are given the chance to build yourself a presentation profile. This is like your online business card or a resume that can be browsed by hundreds of employers.

You need to create a nice description of yourself, list the companies that you have worked for, all your skills, your experience, etc. You are also advised to attach a photo, so that every employer can see the person behind those words as well. Just keep it nice and simple, bid on different jobs, and wait for the response.

Especially in periods when your freelancing jobs are scarce, you can invest some quality time to search on the crowdsourcing portals for potential clients. Also, be daring and mention your price range (per hour, per task, etc.), so that the employer knows these things from the beginning. This way, you will avoid private messages asking for the price and a lot of back & forth emailing.

Crowdsourcing Portals Compendium – so that you pick what you like best

For Freelancers only:

Image Source

  • Spudaroo – web content, press release, sales writing. For a well written business plan, you can take home $1,500
  • Chum Bonus – project managers, freelance architect jobs, software development, programming, etc.
  • Top Coder – this is the place where freelancers compete in graphic design or software competitions.
  • Human Grid – content writing jobs, translating tasks, SEO related jobs in general. Easy sign-up process, and in times when your freelancing projects are few, take advantage of these extra dollars.

In addition to earning some extra money, crowdsourcing portals are also a great way of learning new things. Even though you accept being paid less, you can learn a lot of extra skills in here (for example WP publishing, proofreading skills, audio transcription, using efficiently the keywords within a text, etc.)
