Crowdsourcing vs. Traditional Logo Design

In: 99designs|crowdsourcing material|musings

24 Feb 2011

Can you feel it?  There is a shift going on with the online logo design industry – It’s not just me!

People are beginning to look more and more to crowdsourced solutions for getting things done and a heated debate has erupted within the design industry as it slowly gets turned on its head.

The traditional, ‘old school’ designers have been up in arms over the disruptive business model for years… and can we blame them? In the ‘old boys’ eyes, they can see their old world disintegrating thanks to the new kids on the block like 99designs, but the thing they don’t see is that companies like these are legitimate, are here to stay and are only getting started. These people need to get their heads round the fact that evolution means necessary change for survival: adapt or perish.

You see a lot of back and forth around the subject, but I wanted to take a look at the major difference between the two different types of services (new-school vs old-school) and the benefits that each brings to the most important person in the equation: the customer. The man with the cash.

Old-School: Engaging a Freelancer

Everybody knows the traditional freelancing model. You need some design work. You find a person you think has a decent portfolio, discuss your project with them and allow them to go off and create some basic sketches / ideas for your brand (usually 2-3 different concepts). You work through the design, let it evolve to a point where you are happy, then you get given your new logo and move onto the next project. This is the way in which designers have worked for decades.

Now from a consumers point of view, let’s break down a few key elements of working with an individual designer:

  • You are locked into working with an individual, who’s ideas may not match your own.
  • Your designer may or may not have the creative experience to be able to develop a logo which you LOVE!
  • You will generally only receive a few concepts, which you may or may not like.

Basically, the way I see it, you may be limited in your choice and by the designers current creative inspiration. Not all designers will have ideas which match your vision or that properly matches with your brand.

New-School:  Crowdsourcing Services

Crowdsourcing: Creation of a project to fulfil a need where the collective crowd submits work, answers the question and provides the solution as a collaboration.

There are a whole range of services online these days that use the concept of ‘crowdsourcing’ to get work done.  Using our logo design example, you post a project with information on your company and the brief about what you do, your brand and other key elements. Designers from all over the world can then submit ideas, you review the ideas, refine the best ones and then choose a winner.

As a business owner, the biggest problem in working with a single designer (listed above) is that you are limited by the ideas of a single individual. Sometimes it may be their mood, other times the lack of inspiration. Whatever the reason, it can mean that getting your logo designed can be an experience you wish you hadn’t gone through. Crowdsourcing your logo design on the other hand, has a whole range of advantages.

  • Receive potentially hundreds of individual designs from independent, creative designers.
  • You choose which designs to refine and who to work with based on each designs merit.
  • You determine how much you want to offer as a prize to the winning designer before you launch your contest.
  • You can choose multiple winners if you like more than one design.
  • You get your logo in your time frame

From a business point of view, it seems like a no brainer as crowdsourcing empowers the small business owner to get more choice on a fixed budget.

The original and largest crowdsourced logo design service out there is 99designs, which seems to be by far the market leader for both quality and the volume of contests. Estimates put them at between 65 and 70% of the entire logo design market, so its not surprising that they have been used by over 66,000 small business owners in getting design work on a budget.

The Final Round Up

Looking at New-School vs Old-School, its easy to see why many businesses are moving away from the old model of design. Using crowdsourcing takes a great deal of the worry and uncertainty out of the process of acquiring quality design work as business owners can now budget better, see a whole lot more for the money and work within their time limits.
