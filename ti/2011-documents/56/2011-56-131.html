Crowdsourcing: Jill Sobule’s Next Album is Listener-Funded

By Deidre Sullivan

File under: Crowdsourcing / Comments

NPR looks at the frontiers of crowdsourcing in a podcast about how musician, Jill Sobule, turns to her fans for support.

Some background from NPR:

In another example of just how upended the traditional music industry has become, Sobule’s fundraising scheme worked. In less than two months, she says, she raised $75,000 ‚Äî enough to record, promote and tour for a new album. She says that in 53 days the donations ranged from $5 to much more, from well over 500 people in 44 states, the District of Columbia and 11 foreign countries.

But the point wasn’t just money. “They don’t just give,” Sobule says. “They get!”

For $10 ‚Äî Unpolished Rock (but with potential) Level ‚Äî the supporter is entitled to a free digital download of the album. At the $500 Gold Level, Sobule promises to record an instrumental theme song in the supporter’s name. One supporter from the United Kingdom, Jo, donated at the highest, Weapons-Grade Plutonium Level, an investment of at least $10,000. For that money, Sobule says, she’s singing a duet with Jo.
