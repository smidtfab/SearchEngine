Search Style Sheets: Your Photos, Your Looks

Crowd-Sourcing Style Sites Slowly Replace Personal Taste

by Rika Nurrahmah | 2:23 pm, July 8th, 2010

Constructive criticism is equally as important as personal taste, especially when, for example, you’re trying to figure out whether a LBD or a loose shirt over a pair of cinched khaki shorts would be most appropriate for your friend’s gallery opening. But after getting nothing but nods from friends, and your mother telling you, “You’re beautiful on the inside!”, in can be hard to separate truth from fiction. Thanks to the wonderful invention of the internet, an honest opinion is just a photo upload away.

According to the New York Times, online “crowd-sourcing” for fashion opinions has become a de riguer practice for people of all spectrums — from those for whom the art of dressing is their job to those who just want to look good on a date. If you need an entourage of unbiased fashion consultants or a Rachel Zoe figure in your life without the pricey tag value, websites like Fashism and Go Try It On will get the job done.

These websites allow you to upload a photo of your current look, discuss it, ask questions, and ultimately receive feedback in a matter of minutes. Approval percentage and comments are given on Fashism, while feedback of “wear it” or “change it” is given on Go Try It On.

The creators of each website strive for the same general goal: getting the advice you need without the snarky remarks. Marissa Evans, founder of Go Try It On, states that the website is “for people who want a quick second opinion” that is “helpful, not hurtful.” Fashism’s Brooke Moreland states that users, including herself, are “not professing to be fashion experts.” Rather, they offer a real life, down-to-earth view on the outfit.

While we value the safety net these sites provide from the “Fashion Don’t” list, constant usage would leave little leg room for an opinion that’s equally, if not, more valuable: personal taste.
