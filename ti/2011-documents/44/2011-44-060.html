Human Computation (a Google TechTalk)

Lars Rasmusson suggested seeing a Google TechTalk on Google Video: Human Computation by Luis Von Ahn.

Luis co-invented the now ubiquitous CAPTCHAs.

The point is that there are many problems that (most) people can easily solve that are still difficult for computers, such as reading the above letters.


In the talk, Luis shows how this human problem solving ability can be harnessed and exploited through special computer games, games with a purpose. One example is the ESP game.



a two-player game, the objective of which is to guess what words your partner is typing for each image. The human computation performed is the labeling of images. The game encourages players to name all relevant labels by making previously identified labels into taboo words.


This work is by no means new. I heard Luis give a keynote on this topic at IJCAI-03. But the TechTalk adds more games, showing new ways to exploit human computation. And if you haven't heard him before, don't miss him now. Luis is an excellent speaker and the talk well worth an hour of time.
