Pitfalls of crowdsourcing

The search for missing millionaire aviator Steve Fossett — an archetypal needle-in-a-haystack quest that involved scouring 17,000 square miles — seemed a ready-made opportunity to demonstrate the power of crowdsourcing.

And Amazon’s Mechanical Turk recruited 50,000 volunteers to eyeball the most up-to-date satellite images of the vast search area.

But Fossett has never been found and now, Wired reports, those involved in the search think tapping the masses was not only futile but may actually have hindered the offline search, according to a Civil Air Patrol official.

The biggest stumbling blocks, Turkers tell Wired, appear to be a lack of communication, coordination and feedback that left them pondering questions like, “Did anyone else flag this smudge already?” “Has anyone else already found a useful clue someplace else?”

In other words, they wanted more of the expensive, labor-intensive organizational work that automated crowdsourcing is supposed to do away with.
