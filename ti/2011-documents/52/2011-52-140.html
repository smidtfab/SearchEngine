Crowdsourcing Business Plans

By benjones | Views: 22 | 0 Comments | Rating: 0 |

Crowdsourcing is a relatively new business concept where entrepreneurs take advantage of the masses to generate, cultivate, and execute a business idea. Crowdsourcing is enabled by social media and other means that bring common people together.

Crowdsourcing through Social Media

So how do you crowdsource your idea? The first step is to engage the crowd through social media. By using Twitter, Facebook, and other social media outlets, you can announce your idea or question to your network. You may want to incentivize your offer by offering a gift card or bragging rights to those with the best ideas. Be careful to not give away the secrets of your business idea, but give enough detail that your followers can clearly understand the business concept.

Crowdsourcing through Consumer Research

Marketing research is one of the oldest forms of crowdsourcing. Reserchers regularly crowdsource ideas from the general population by conducting surveys and focus groups. If you have a partially-developed business concept, you can lean on the expertise of the masses by explaining your business idea to a small focus group of consumers who would be relevant to your business. Use their feedback to tweak the concept, the competitive advantage, the messaging, the positioning, and the overall operation of the company. When crowdsourcing through consumer research you should be prepared to make judgment calls. Not every suggestion is a good one, so take each comment with a grain of salt.

Crowdsourcing through Personal Networks

Which people are the most likely to listen to your business idea and give you feedback? Your friends and family. Reach out to your loved ones and ask them to be consultants to your business. You will explain your business to them very clearly and then ask them to explain the company back to you. By including them in the creation phase of the process, they will be more likely to offer up good ideas later when it comes time to creating the business plan.
