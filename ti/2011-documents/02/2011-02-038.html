Crowd-sourcing for Community Building

Tuesday, October 19, 2010

5:30 PM

Selected By: user 10036437

Centre for Social Innovation

215 Spadina Avenue Toronto, ON

Selected By: user 10036437
43 NetSquared Community Members went
user 10036437 added this Meetup on Sep 21, 2010

Price: CAD5.00 per person(refund policy)

Crowd-sourcing, community building, wisdom of crowds, user generated, whatever you want to call it.

The essence of crowd-sourcing is knowing that your audience is more intelligent collectively than isolated. Can you ask your community to help? It could be as simple as answering a poll in your newsletter or getting your community to vote for you to get a corporate grant  either way you are using your skills of crowd-sourcing. Your community is there for you; let them fight your battles. Just as you provide a service, your community appreciates that and is happy to share their powers too.
Discover how to use creative crowd-sourcing within your non-profit.

Speakers:

Heather Leson
Heather Leson is a technologist and crisis communicator. Bridging these two worlds is her calling. After acquiring education in politics and library information technology, Heather worked in Internet organizations (IP networking and Internet applications) with a focus on technical incident, problem management and emergency response. She is co-leads the Community Working Group for CrisisCommons and co-founded CrisisCamp Toronto. She blogs at crisiscommons.org and textontechs.com.

Julie Tyios
Julie is an entrepreneur, tech and gaming nerd, and the founder of Red Juice Media, an online marketing firm. She works with small businesses to build their presence online, and also provides social media and communications consulting. In March 2010 she was appointed to the TTC Customer Service Advisory Panel to examine the TTCs communications methods and provide recommendations on how to improve the customer experience. She is also the marketing and community manager of Vestiigo.com, a Canadian start-up career community. Julie is also an avid writer and photographer, specializing in commercial and wedding photography. If shes not taking pictures, shes exploring her creative side through writing, painting, and huge love of experimental cooking (in a condo-sized kitchen).

Darren O'Donnell
Darren ODonnell is a novelist, essayist, playwright, director, designer, performer and artistic director of Mammalian Diving Reflex. His books include: Social Acupuncture, which argues for an aesthetics of civic engagement. Mammalian Diving Reflex is a research-art atelier dedicated to investigating the social sphere, always on the lookout for contradictions to whip into aesthetically scintillating experiences, producing one-off events, theatre-based performance, videos, installation, theoretical texts and community happenings. Past work includes: Eat the Street, Haircuts by Children, The Childrens Choice Awards, Slow Dance With Teacher, A Suicide-Site Guide to the City, Monster Makers, The Best Sex Ive Ever Had and Old Women Shooting Guns, and Nuit Market . Mammalian Diving Reflexs work has been presented around the world in Birmingham, Bologna, Brisbane, Calgary, Chicago, Cork, Derry, Edmonton, Greensboro, Kitchener, Lahore, London, Los Angeles, Melbourne, Milan, Mumbai, Montreal, Newcastle, New York, Norwich, Oldenburg, Oslo, Ottawa, Perth, Portland, Regina, Sydney, Tasmania, Terni, Trondheim, Vancouver and Victoria.
