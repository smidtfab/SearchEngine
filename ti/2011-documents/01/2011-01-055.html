All music is Fan Funded - So why not try something different?

Benji Rogers at 11:36 21 December, 2010

All music is Fan Funded – So why not try something different?

Since PledgeMusic began, the team and I have spent probably way too many hours trying to explain to artists firstly, why we don’t consider ourselves a “Fan/Crowd Funding” company but a direct to fan engagement platform, and secondly, why you should never just flat out ask your fans to fund your music. Or in other words just ask them for money. It occurred to me this morning though, that if we break it down to it’s nuts and bolts, all music is Fan/Crowd funded. It’s just a question, in most cases, of when the fan funds it.

In the case of getting a record deal, the label advances the artist the money to make the record and then the crowd/fans fund the repayments back to the label/artist. Sometimes there is even a pre-sale which means that the fans are asked to put money into the label/artists pockets before they get anything and this too I consider to be fan funding. So let’s be clear here: Fans fund albums and tours. It’s just a question of wether there is a loan up front, or not and who assumes the risk.

To me the proposition needs to change. The terms “Fund my album” and “Buy my album” mean the same thing. It’s just a question of when you get the goods. To be clear if you want to ask your fans to give you money you can just put a PayPal button on your website or go to one of the numerous available crowd funding/investment sites and ask them to donate or in some cases invest in your cause? The concept of giving more money and the reward being what you have purchased always seemed to stand at a strange angle to me as a purchaser, as how can what I have paid for truly be considered a reward? So what else can you offer your fans?

Don’t ask your fans to give you money.

Offer them ability to participate in an experience and not just a sale.

Don’t make it about funding.

Make it about what and who you are and not just the products that you create.

Don’t sell products and call them rewards.

Offer experiences and access. Create true value in the way the fans can participate.

Don’t up-sell.

Make the ways in which people can participate fair and focus on the participation/experience being the true value for money.

Don’t look desperate.

Don’t put your hand out and beg.

Make what ever you are offering your fans the most exciting, creative, engaging and participatory experience that you can come up with – i.e. imagine what you would want to see from your favourite artist and offer your fans better.

Put yourself for two minutes into the shoes of fans. All day long they are offered a million ways to part with their money. “Fund my new album” “Buy tickets to my show,” “Buy our new T-Shirt,” “Fund our tour.” The sheer number of people and parties competing for your fans ears and wallets is staggering and overwhelming to them. As music sales plummet, and as tens of thousands of bands attempt to ask their fans for money in an ever dizzying number of ways you need to ask yourself each and every day: what will make people want to be a part of what I do? If you focus only on bigger and better ways to sell then you are just contributing to the noise.

It’s loud out there.

All music is Fan Funded – So why not try something different?
