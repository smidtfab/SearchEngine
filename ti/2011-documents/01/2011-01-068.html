    4. What’s it?
      Amazon Mechanical Turk is a crowdsourcing Internet marketplace that enables computer programmers (Requesters) to coordinate the use of human intelligence to perform tasks which computers are unable to do.
    5. https://www.mturk.com/
    6. Workers get paid to answer stuff.
    7. Requesters pay to ask stuff.
    8. Why use it?
      Advantages of Mechanical Turk*:
      • low cost ($0.10 per 60-second task)
      • subject pool size
      • subject pool diversity
      • faster theory/experiment cycle
      * Mason, W. and Siddarth, S. 2009. “Conducting Behavioral Research on Amazon’s Mechanical Turk.” Yahoo! Research.

    10. Human Intelligence Tasks (HITs)
      Requesters can specify:
      • task
      • keywords
      • expiration date
      • reward
      • time allotted
      • worker qualifications
      • location
      • approval rating
    11. Example HITs:
      “Identify forward-facing pictures of dogs”
      “Find and enter a business address”
      “How attractive are these items?”
      “Choose the best category for this product”
      “Read a set of Tweets and decide if they describe an event”
    12. Designing HITs
      Options:
      Use template or code within Mechanical Turk
      • requires basic HTML
      Link to external survey
      • need some kind of verification input on Mechanical Turk side after Workers complete external survey

    15. Test your HITs
      Use the Mechanical Turk Requester sandbox
      • view your published your HIT before releasing it to Workers
      • check for functionality in all browsers
    16. Help Workers work efficiently
      Don’t make them scroll and click a lot
      Set pages to open in new windows
    17. Manage Worker accuracy
      Reject bad work
      Reward good work with bonuses
      Filter Workers by qualifications
      • design a test for specific knowledge
      • location
      • approvals (percentage or number)
      • adult content

    19. Payment information
      HITs must be prepaid to a Mechanical Turk account
      • Amazon collects 10% on top of what you pay to Workers
      • Amazon collects 10% of any bonuses you grant
      • Payment to Workers can be in money or Amazon credit
      You’re ultimately only charged for approved HITs, but you’re liable for the amount equal to whatever 100% approved HITs would cost.
      You’ll have to deal with tax stuff if Workers do enough work for you to meet the IRS threshold for taxable income.
      Click for prepaid terms and tax information.
    20. What You Can’t Do
      Not warn Workers about explicit or offensive content
      • label HIT appropriately
      • require and check for Adult Content Qualification in order to complete AND preview HIT
      Collect personally identifiable information
      Ask for this:
      • e-mail address
      • registration at another site
      • downloading of software
      Click to view complete policies.

      Tversky and Kahneman:

      • anchoring – “African countries problem”
