It must be pointed out here that SDSS differs from other space observatories such as HUBBLE, HERSCHEL, SPITZER, etc in that it is a large scale astronomical surveyÂ  instrument. It's main purpose is to cover as many objects and the largest possible sky area in the shortest possible time. Therefore, the images are usually not as sharp as its space observatory counter parts which are used to home in on specific objects of interest for close-up and detailed study. However, there are exceptionally spectacular images on SDSS as well. For example, the SDSS image of the NEEDLE GALAXY ( NGC 4565 ) is reputed to be better than the HUBBLE image on the same object but this is more an exception than the rule ( See images below -Â  the first one is from the HUBBLE HERITAGE SITE while the second one is from SDSS Database ). One must also appreciate that this trade off between quantity ( SDSS ) and quality ( HUBBLE ) is justified by the far lower cost of operation ( including repairs and maintenance ) for ground base instruments than for the space observatories and in particular the extensive survey mission of the SDSS.
