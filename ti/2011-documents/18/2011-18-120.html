Excalibur chosen to supply information retrieval solutions to six prime contractors in $100...

Publication: Business Wire
Date: Friday, September 6 1996
Share:
Print
More

VIENNA, Va.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Sept. 6, 1996--Excalibur Technologies Corp. (NASDAQ:EXCA), a leading provider of software solutions for electronic information retrieval, Friday announced that it will provide a wide range of COTS (commercial off-the-shelf) and other high-performance information retrieval and document management products to six prime contractors selected on the newly-awarded National Institutes of Health's (NIH) National Information Technology Acquisition and Assessment Center (NITAAC) ImageWorld Contract.

Excalibur's software solutions may be purchased on prime contracts through BTG, Unisys, Lockheed, and EDS; 8(a) purchases are available through Multimax and Universal Hi-Tech Development.

With a focus on providing complete solutions for information retrieval and document management over intranets and the Internet, Excalibur will supply RetrievalWare(TM), Electronic Filing Software (EFS)(TM) and several bundled solutions.

RetrievalWare is one of the first unified family of software components developed to significantly improve the productivity and lower the costs of information management systems.

Excalibur Security Servers and Visual RetrievalWare(TM), an image "plug and play" feature for photos, video, facial prints, and fingerprints, are a few of the optional Excalibur add-on products also offered by the named NIH ImageWorld contractors.

"In a recently conducted IDC survey of federal Internet usage, IDC has learned that most of the business processes conducted by federal employees concern the collection and gathering of information over the Internet or World Wide Web, therefore making the need for retrieval tools indispensable," said Vicky Page, senior research analyst at International Data Corp. (IDC).

"Our line of information retrieval and document management products help turn information into knowledge," said Tom Polivka, Excalibur's director of federal operations.

"Many federal agencies are already using our products to convert their paper overloads into databases that are easily searchable and shared among several remote locations. We offer the federal government a broad range of search and retrieval capabilities across LANs and WANs, the Internet, Intelink or agency intranets."

The ImageWorld contract provide Image Processing Resources and Services for the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and other Government Agencies.

The estimated value of the IDIQ (indefinite delivery, indefinite quantity) contract is nearly $100 million over the next five years and will be shared by multiple awarded vendors. Information concerning the contract is available at http://www.nih.gov:80/od/oirm.

Excalibur Technologies Corp.

Excalibur Technologies Corp. (NASDAQ:EXCA) has set the standard for excellence in information retrieval since 1980, leading the industry in delivering high-performance search and retrieval solutions to the online, federal and corporate markets.

Only Excalibur delivers semantic toolkits and imaging applications based on both pattern-recognition and semantic network searching, which leverages the underlying patterns of digital information and the actual meaning of words. RetrievalWare provides the first set of tools optimized for creating retrieval solutions for text, images and other forms of digital media.
