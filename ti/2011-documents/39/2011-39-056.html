USPS turns to Prova to crowdsource their Priority Mail Box Design

Jul 22, 2010 by David Bratvold

Will your design be on the next USPS Priority Mail Box?

The United States Postal Service in Hawaii has selected the online community of designers at Prova to crowdsource the design of a “Season’s Greetings from Hawaii” logo to be printed on the Hawaii Priority Mail Large Flat Rate Boxes. A new design contest via Prova’s online platform was created to satisfy the Post Office’s marketing, branding, and advertising needs.  Open to the public & USPS employees, this contest was created to update a holiday image that appears on all boxes shipped from Hawaii.

The United States Postal Service (USPS) in Honolulu approached Prova to engage the design community there with the design project. The goal of the USPS campaign is to “inspire Postal Service customers to choose the Large Flat Rate Box for shipping packages worldwide”.

“We liked Prova’s system because we’ll get a wider submission base with Prova, than simply getting ideas from our employees. You just put down what you want and designers from all over are going to be able to submit their designs.”
-Wong, USPS Retail Manager

Prova takes advantage of virtually untapped skills and resources via the web. Tens of thousands of talented artists and designers can be engaged using Prova as a platform for assignments – as well as for promoting their work. Prova’s CEO and Founder had this to say about the contest; “Crowdsourcing is beginning to come into its own because of the advent and adaptation of digital technologies. We are obviously thrilled the digital design community has this opportunity to satisfy a tangible need of a global concern such as the USPS.”

More details about the Post Office’s design contest can be found at Prova along with the current entries.
