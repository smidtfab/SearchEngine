Crowd-sourcing a Book on Law School Lives: Tell Your Story Before Its Lost

Share Tweet
28 March 2011 View Comments

 

 

By Ramanuj Mukherjee

 

Law schools are fascinating places. There are so many things happening every day that are not just everyday happenings. But as years pile up, memories fade and we lose track. Stories that fascinate one generation of law schoolites are lost to another generation.

 

Much of the ordinary life in a law school, the moots, the internships, interviews and vacation schemes remain mystery to the outside world. Law schools have fairy tales, legends of hard fought battles, adventures that have taken people through continents.

 

Each story has its own share of humour, moments of nostalgia and fun.

 

There are stories of self discovery and snatching victories against unbelievable odds, as well as stories of disillusionment, and how people lost their souls to law school.

 

Don’t let the stories be lost again. It has been almost two and half decades since the first law school came about. It is time to unravel some of the mystery called law school for the rest of the world.

 

It is time we preseve the most remarkable memories and share them with rest of the law school community, and with other students, colleges, and with all those for whom law school life is a mysterious void. Law schools have extraordinary stories. It is time to tell them.

 

Some of us, students from 3 different law schools, have started to identify and collect the most extraordinary and interesting stories from law schools.

 

Stories of students travelling on bicycle from Kolkata to Hyderabad, hosting the first international law students’ conference, exotic internships, hostel politics, experience of working at the United Nations, even top Indian law firms, project scams, organising fests that did not go exactly according to plans, batch trips – anything with a story value and a connection with the law school life are on the hunting list.

 

Keep in mind that the range we are dealing is very broad and inclusive.

 

The stories can be about anything starting from internships, classes, funny teachers, vacation schemes, college fests, project making, law school parties, stories of extraordinary success or tragedy to adventures or anything else you can think of.

 

Do you think you know a very interesting story that people will want to read and say “Wow! Even that happens!”? Then you have a story we are looking for.

 

It is not necessary that the story is about you – as long as you know it exists, you can tell us. If you want to write it, go ahead. If not, we’ll find someone to interview you and write the story down.

 

Since it is a collaborative project by nature, we need lots and lots of people to join and work together to identify the most exciting stories, and then write and edit them. At the end of all these, a book will be published, in which your efforts shall be duly acknowledged.

 

Who do you think might want to read the book?

Alumni, to read and remember the exploits at law school they witnessed and participated in, or just missed.

Current students, to know how law schools are elsewhere.

Law aspirants, because they want to know what law schools are like, what does spending 5 years in a law school entail?

And generally, anyone looking for exciting, energetic stories will want to read a book like this.

 

To participate meaningfully in the project, you can do any of the following:

1. Tell us about the greatest stories you know about and those who in your opinion knows enough/ will write these stories well.

2. Write a story yourself and send it across.

3. Even if you don’t want to write, or do not have the time but have some amazing stories to share, just tell us.

We shall hook you up with a fabulous writer who will interview you and write the story for you.

People who do any of the above will be duly acknowledged in the book.

 

Please send in an email at ramanujwrites[at]gmail.com as soon as you can, because we can accommodate only a limited number of stories.

 

If you have the most amazing story but you are too late in letting us know, we shall feel very sad about missing a good story but will be able to do very little. You can even just drop us a mail and let us know you are interested, and we shall follow you up for the rest!

 

If you are from the following law schools, you can contact the person mentioned. For rest of the law schools, we still need representatives. Please let us know if you are interested.

 

NLU, Delhi: Riju Dev

NLU, Jodhpur: Divya Seth

NUJS, Kolkata: Ramanuj Mukherjee
