IPhone Apps By Crowdsourcing's Labotec Angel Funded


Miami-based Labotec has landed a round of funding from Kima Ventures, although the amount was undisclosed.

We are not fans of the model. Labotec has a form on their site seeking iPhone ideas from "inspirers." Labotec will judge the best ideas and create iPhone apps out of them. Inspirers put nothing up-front but earn 50% of profits after Labotec earns the first $25,000.

What we don't like about the model is that we think ideas are cheap - execution and brands are what matters. We don't think that brands will become inspirers so that means that Labotec will pay 50% of their profits to people who toss over ideas. They may not even be good ideas after all, Labotec will have to decide that and then its up to them to execute. It seems like Labotec has set themselves up for a bad deal.

Labotec has launched 3 applications so far - iSOS for Android, iMove2Music and FakeSMS for iPhone. They plan and 20 more by the end of this year. You make the call if these are good app ideas or not:

+ iSOS app can be activated in case of an emergency. Just by one click on the icon, iSOS will send emergency voice, texts messages, emails, FB and twitter updates to 5 of your chosen contacts. Plus, it automatically calls 911 (112 UE) and sends your GPS-positioning so that people can find you and help you easily & efficiently.

+ iMove2music is a musical application that enables you to enjoy your favorite tracks in a dynamic and original way on your iPhone or iPod Touch. As long as you move, dance, walk or run, your music follows your every move! When you stop, the song you hear does the same. Once you are back in motion, the music starts to play again.

+ Fake SMS has you create fake text conversations from fake contacts (eg : Barack Obama) or real friends.
