4 Key Questions to ask Crowdfunding Founders – The Trust Factor

I was asked by one of my Twitter followers, what is the most important thing to look for when choosing a crowdfunding platform for your project? This has come up before in my discussions with some of the crowdfunding Founders. As consumers, our greatest fear when dealing with investing, donating or managing money online is TRUST. As artists, entrepreneurs and inventors we have the same fears; who do we TRUST with our visions? So, on that note, here are the top three questions you should ask of any crowdfunding platform:

  1. What percentage of the money raised goes to the platform management? We should know how much we are paying the broker (platform) to help us raise money;
  2. How many successful fundings (100% funded projects) has the crowdfunding platform completed? Do they have a vibrant community that will find and rally around your project?
  3. Can the platform demonstrate that their listed projects have been completed? What percentage were completed?
  4. Are the completed projects of a quality that satisfies you? Or you could ask, are the projects of a standard relevant to the dollars raised? The platform’s projects will be a reflection on your project.

All of these question address the level of trust that you and your project supporters should demand. I would also encourage you to engage the Founders of these platforms on Twitter or on their blogs.
