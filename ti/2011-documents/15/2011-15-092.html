Is the translation crowdsourcing market getting too crowded?

Feb 2
Translation Websites

Crowdsourcing is one of the hot trends in the translation industry. Many industry insiders and translation companies are thinking of ways to use the power of the crowd to lower translation prices. But recently, the word ‘crowd’ brings another kind of thought to my mind. As in ‘crowded.’ It seems that every other day another company is jumping on the online translation bandwagon and offering low-cost translation services. And most of these companies are small enterprises or startups. Is the market for online translations large enough to make it worthwhile for all of these companies? And if the market is so large, why aren’t the large LSPs getting into it? Does anybody have answers to this? I’d like to hear them.

There was an article in CNET yesterday about a new translation crowdsourcing company named Serv.io. But this company is not really new. It used to be called Translation Zen and has been rebranded (does anyone know why?). I wrote a blog post about Translation Zen last year.

Here are some other online translation companies that I know of. Please let me know if I left any out.

  • Babylon. The maker of dictionary and translation software offers quick turnaround human translation services.
  • Onehourtranslation
  • Mygengo. I wrote a blog post last December about this company.
  • ABBYY
  • Smartling
  • ToLingo
  • Translate this now
  • Speak Like
  • Straker Software. I wrote a blog post last December about this company.

    Hi Dave,

    The change from the “Zen” branding to Servio was a marketing decision.

    Servio is powered by CloudCrowd, our proprietary workspace platform. We have built a number of services based on CloudCrowd, including editing, translation, and content development/enhancement.

    Our services were originally branded with the “Zen” mark (EditZen, TranslationZen), but when we started to branch into additional services, we found that the Zen brand wasn’t scaling as well as we wanted it to when we wanted to add new domains.
