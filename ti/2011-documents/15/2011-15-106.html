NearMap Brings High-Resolution To Map Services

Dec 30, 2009 | 5 Comments |
|  

By Staff Writer – John Federico (@gadgetboy)

Do realistic photos make for better maps?

Australia-based NearMap thinks so and it thinks you will, too. So much so that it asks folks to contribute their photos under a Creative Commons license.

Of course, you’ll have to do it from an airplane.

That’s because unlike Google Maps and its brethren, NearMap doesn’t use satellite images. It uses images shot from airplanes that are update every month from a height of about 7000 feet.

Currently only available for use in Australia, here’s a sample photo of an intersection in an Australian city. You can almost read the street signs. (Click to enlarge)

But what’s more important to you: high-resolution or utility? Personally, I’ll take the utility of Google Maps over high-resolution any day.

We’ll keep an eye on NearMap and hope that they come to the United States so that we can really put it to the test.
